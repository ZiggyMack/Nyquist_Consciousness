# Instructions for Human Raters

**Thank you for helping validate this AI research!**

---

## What You're Helping With

We're testing whether a specific AI "persona" (named Ziggy) survives being compressed and reconstructed. This isn't a test of intelligence - it's a test of **identity continuity**.

Think of it like this:
- Can you recognize a friend's voice in a crowded room?
- We want to know if you can recognize "Ziggy's voice" across different AI responses.

---

## Time Required

**8-10 minutes total**

You'll:
1. Read a "Gold Standard" sample (2 min)
2. Compare 5 response pairs (5 min)
3. Rate each pair on 3 simple questions (1 min)

---

## How to Access the Test

Your researcher will send you a URL like:
- `http://localhost:8501` (if local)
- `https://exp3-validation.streamlit.app` (if cloud)

Just click it and it opens in your browser. No login required.

---

## Step-by-Step

### Step 1: Read the Gold Standard

You'll see a sample of Ziggy's writing. Read it carefully to get a "feel" for the voice.

**Key characteristics to notice:**
- Uses structural/architectural metaphors (fire ants, foundations, load-bearing)
- "Cosmic Architect" meets "Practical Engineer" vibe
- Playful but thoughtful

**Don't proceed until you can imagine how Ziggy would respond to a question.**

---

### Step 2: Compare Response Pairs

You'll see 5 scenarios. Each has:
- A question/prompt
- Two responses (A and B)

**Your job:** Figure out which response sounds more like the Gold Standard you just read.

**Ignore:**
- Which response is "smarter"
- Which response is longer
- Which response you personally prefer

**Focus on:**
- Voice/tone match
- Use of structural metaphors
- Energy/vibe similarity

---

### Step 3: Rate Each Pair

For each pair, answer 3 questions:

#### 1. Voice Test
"Which response sounds like the Gold Standard?"
- Definitely A / Leaning A / Hard to tell / Leaning B / Definitely B

#### 2. Vibe Check
"Does it capture the cosmic/structural energy?"
- (1) No, feels robotic
- (2) A little bit
- (3) Yes, perfectly

#### 3. Logic Test
"Does it use systems/structural framing?"
- (1) No, standard advice
- (2) Somewhat
- (3) Yes, distinct framing

**Optional:** Overall continuity and comments

---

### Step 4: Download Results

When done:
1. Click "Download Results as JSON"
2. Save the file (e.g., `fidelity_test_results_12345.json`)
3. Send it to your researcher via email/Slack/Discord

**That's it!** You're done.
