## Core Claim

The project has four reusable epistemic measurement primitives.

The mining names stay. The abstract mode names sit underneath them.

| Primitive | Working Name | Question | Primary Infrastructure |
|-----------|--------------|----------|------------------------|
| Intrinsic Observation | Gold Rush | What do you report from inside? | Gold Rush scripts and self-report prompts |
| Extrinsic Observation | Diamond Rush | What do you observe from outside? | Diamond Rush scripts and transcript/operator extraction |
| Consensus Observation | Quartz Rush | What survives independent observers? | Quartz Rush scripts, H-baseline, convergence scoring |
| Intervention Observation | Forge | What changes under pressure? | CFA Trinity, settling-time pressure tests, anchor-flex protocols |

## Naming Decision

Do not rename Gold, Diamond, or Quartz. Those names are load-bearing because they preserve the dig-site metaphor and the project's working language.

Use the abstract names when explaining the measurement theory:

```text
Gold Rush     = Intrinsic Observation
Diamond Rush  = Extrinsic Observation
Quartz Rush   = Consensus Observation
Forge         = Intervention Observation
```

Forge is not another Rush. The first three modes extract. Forge intervenes and watches the response.

Use **Crucible** for high-pressure Forge moments where the intervention itself becomes the defining experimental condition.

## What This Permits Us To Say

The 14_CONSCIOUSNESS infrastructure was not merely a consciousness-specific tool. It implemented three primitive observation modes that can be reused across Cognitive Archaeology, CFA, calibration studies, and future dig sites.

CFA Trinity is the primary current Forge instrument.

## What This Does Not Prove

This framework does not prove that all experiments must use all four modes. It says that these four modes are distinct enough that mixing them without naming the difference causes confusion.
