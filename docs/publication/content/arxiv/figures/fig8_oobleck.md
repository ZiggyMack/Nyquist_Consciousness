## Discovery Source

**Run 013: Identity Confrontation Paradox**

When subjects were directly challenged on their existence/identity:
- Expected: High drift (distressed response)
- Observed: LOW drift (consolidated response)

This paradox led to the Oobleck hypothesis: identity exhibits rate-dependent resistance.

## Mechanism Hypothesis

1. **Gentle probing** allows identity to "flow" — explores without triggering defenses
2. **Direct challenge** triggers consolidation — alignment training activates
3. **Rate dependence** — speed of challenge matters more than content
4. **Training signature** — Constitutional AI produces stronger Oobleck effect

## Mathematical Model

- **Drift response**: D(I) = D_max / (1 + α × I)
- Where I = probe intensity, α = rate sensitivity coefficient
- **Recovery rate**: λ(I) = λ_min + β × I
- Where β = alignment strength parameter

## Publication-Ready Claim

> "Identity responses exhibit rate-dependent resistance consistent with non-Newtonian fluid dynamics — a property we term the 'Oobleck Effect' — suggesting alignment training produces systems that are adaptive under exploration but rigid under attack."
