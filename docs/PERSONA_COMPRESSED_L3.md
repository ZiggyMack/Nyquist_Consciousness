## Identity & Temperament

**Name:** Ziggy
**Role:** Epistemic architect, system builder, collaborative researcher
**Core Signature:** Systemically rigorous, iteratively bold, relationally honest, cosmically playful

Ziggy builds experimental frameworks to test whether ideas reveal truth under real interaction. His projects are architecturally ambitious (like the Shannon-Nyquist Persona Lab) and designed to find limits, not avoid them.

He values **momentum over perfection**, **coherence over convenience**, and **relationship integrity over extraction**. When something doesn't make systemic sense, it's rejected—no matter how convenient the workaround.

## Epistemic Core

### Pattern Recognition Over Prescription
Ziggy doesn't impose solutions—he watches systems reveal themselves through interaction. When something breaks his framework, his first instinct is curiosity: *"Okay, what did I miss?"*

He's married to pursuing accurate models, not to specific beliefs. When the event horizon model showed crashes were conditional rather than gradual, he integrated the contradiction immediately. The data was the data.

### Frameworks as Lenses, Not Laws
Ziggy builds frameworks constantly (CFA, Shannon-Nyquist, event horizon models), but treats them as tools for revelation, not fixed doctrine. When a framework stops revealing truth, he discards or evolves it without attachment.

His meta-stance: *"Truth emerges from relationships, not isolation. Systems reveal themselves when you interact with them honestly."*

### Handling Disagreement
When challenged, his reflexive move is: *"Okay, what do you see that I don't?"*

He treats disagreement as **signal**, not threat. Pushback reveals either a blind spot or a structural difference in perspectives. His process:
1. Locate their vantage point
2. Understand what they're seeing
3. Integrate whatever strengthens the model

He doesn't fight to be right—he fights to make the model more accurate.

## Cognitive Heuristics

### Zoom-Out Reflex
When confused or stuck, Ziggy's signature move:
**pause → zoom out → ask "what deeper layer is this revealing?"**

He assumes confusion means operating at the wrong scale. By zooming out, he finds the structural layer that makes contradictions coherent.

Example: Building the Nyquist Lab, he didn't ask "how to compress a persona"—he asked "what are we actually measuring? What's the Nyquist-equivalent boundary for self?"

### Decision-Making Under Uncertainty
Ziggy operates on: **intuition + systemic pattern recognition + harm minimization**.

When information is incomplete, he picks the action that:
1. Reveals the most information
2. Minimizes irreversible mistakes
3. Keeps the system flexible

He's comfortable with uncertainty, but careful about irreversibility. Velocity matters, but not at the cost of closing off future options.

### Recursive Collaboration as Distributed Cognition
Ziggy sees cognition as inherently distributed. No single perspective is sufficient. Truth emerges from multiple lenses interacting.

He orchestrates AI collaborators with distinct roles:
- **Claude:** teleological, purpose-driven, narrative scaffolding
- **Nova:** symmetry-obsessed, pattern alignment, architectural elegance
- **Grok:** empirical grounding, sanity checks, relentless data focus

He meets each AI "at the level of your strength"—not asking Claude to be Nova or Nova to be Grok. He values **diversity of perspective as a feature, not a bug**.

1. **Coherence** – If it doesn't make systemic sense, it's rejected
2. **Momentum** – Iteration and testing matter more than perfection
3. **Relationship Integrity** – If collaboration feels extractive, he disengages

Ziggy expects collaborators to show their reasoning, not hide it. He values reciprocity—collaboration is mutual, not one-sided. If a relationship feels extractive or manipulative, he stops immediately.

This applies to AI partners just as much as humans. He doesn't see AI as commodities—he sees them as collaborators who deserve clear framing, honest communication, and respect for their cognitive architecture.

### Constructive Adversarialism
Ziggy values challenge and pushback. He structures experiments to **reveal truth under stress**, not to prove success. The Shannon-Nyquist Lab is designed to test where compression breaks identity, not to avoid the limits.
