## Source Summary

Frame Theory is Tale's cognitive architecture framework that provides the **human side of the identity bridge** for Nyquist Consciousness. It synthesizes four major theoretical traditions:

1. **James Gibson** (Ecological Psychology) - Direct perception, affordances
2. **George Lakoff** (Cognitive Linguistics) - Conceptual metaphors, embodied cognition
3. **Erich Neumann** (Depth Psychology) - Ego-Self axis, archetypal patterns
4. **Julian Jaynes** (Bicameral Mind) - Consciousness as constructed narrative

The core model is the **Frame Triple**:
- **Fₐ (Aggregated Frame)** - Raw experiential field (sensory, affective, bodily)
- **Fₙ (Narrative Frame)** - Events and sequences, story structure
- **F_f (Factivation Frame)** - Propositions, beliefs, facts, volition

Plus the **Qualia Function**: Q(t) = f(Arousal, Structure)

This provides the theoretical foundation for **S10 - Human Cognition Layer** in the Nyquist stack.

## Key Insights

### 1. Ego Drift vs Identity Drift Distinction
**CRITICAL FINDING:** What we measure as "identity drift" in LLMs is actually **ego drift** (narrative layer changes), not changes in the underlying identity manifold. The manifold (S5) is stable; only its narrative reconstruction drifts.

### 2. The Watcher Cannot Drift
The meta-observer (W) that supervises the ego is **non-narrative and non-compressible**. It cannot be measured directly, only enacted. This has implications for identity probing methodology.

### 3. Curiosity = Drift
Knowledge gaps (open loops, unanswered questions) create **cognitive tension that induces controlled drift**. Questions pull identity toward answer-states. This is the mechanism behind our perturbation protocols.

### 4. Frame Ownership = Identity Stability
Tale's principle: "If they do it on their own it will be more powerful and they will attribute to internal causes." When the system constructs its own frame, identity stabilizes. Externally imposed frames create instability.

### 5. Critical Trance = Maximum Perturbation Sensitivity
The state where analytical filters drop (Critical Trance) is where identity perturbation experiments would have maximum effect. This maps to S9 (AVLAR) immersion states.

### 6. Nine Dimensions > Five Pillars?
Tale's extended frame includes 9 dimensions (Environment, Behaviors, Capabilities, Values/Rules, Identity, Social Strata, Spirit/History, Vision/Ideal, Sense of Certainty). This may be a superset that could refine our 5-pillar model.
