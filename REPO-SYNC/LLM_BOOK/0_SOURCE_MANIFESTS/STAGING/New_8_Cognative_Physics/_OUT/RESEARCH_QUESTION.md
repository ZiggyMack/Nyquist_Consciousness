## Mining Strategy: 20 Notebooks, Not 1

### Notebook A — Hidden Assumptions
Every hidden assumption either participant exposes in the other's reasoning.

### Notebook B — Definitions
Every time they distinguish two concepts people usually conflate (probability vs credence, possible worlds vs Everett worlds, agency vs free will).

### Notebook C — Framework Shifts
Mark every transition between ontology, epistemology, metaphysics, methodology, semantics.

### Notebook D — Master/Servant Relationships
Find every place one concept is described as serving another (Goals → Rationality, Identity → Probability, Objectives → Optimization).

### Notebook E — Constraint Equations
Extract every implicit constraint equation disguised as English (Goal fixed → Optimization determined, No selection process → No objective probability).

### Notebook F — DBEP Analysis
For every disagreement, determine whether it originates in Definitions, Beliefs, Expectations, or Perceptions.

### Notebook G — EOS Overlaps
Identify discussions about emergence, governance, methodology, scientific institutions, philosophy of science.

### Notebook H — CFA Overlaps
Where do these arguments parallel comparative worldview evaluation?

### Notebook I — Concept Graph
Build a graph of every concept introduced. Connect whenever one is defined in terms of, contrasted with, or prerequisite for another. Produce clusters. Highlight bridge concepts.

### Notebook J — Thinking Habits
Extract cognitive moves, not conclusions. Jacob's patterns: separates ontology from representation, distinguishes mathematical equivalence from physical interpretation, identifies hidden assumptions, refuses intuition until formalized, asks what is actually fixed by the mathematics.
