## Cognitive Architecture Extraction (CAE) — Emerging Methodology

### The Three Artifacts

This project produces three distinct artifacts, each at a different level:

- **Artifact A:** Barandes' physics (ISP theory, graded realism, interpretation diagnostic)
- **Artifact B:** The cognitive architecture that generated Barandes' physics (operators, heuristics, discovery methods)
- **Artifact C:** The extraction methodology that recovered Artifact B (the Barandes Protocol — replicable for next thinker)

Artifact C has the longest shelf life.

### The 5-Level Stack

Where this work sits relative to Barandes:

| Level | Name | Content | Who Lives Here |
|-------|------|---------|----------------|
| 5 | Physical Theory | ISP, QM, graded realism | Barandes |
| 4 | Representation | Hilbert space ↔ vintage probabilities | Barandes |
| 3 | Conceptual Architecture | Operators, heuristics, evaluation criteria | Barandes (implicit), us (extracting) |
| 2 | Discovery Operators | Subtractive Discovery, Pedagogical Forcing, etc. | Us (formalizing) |
| 1 | Epistemic Operating System | Reusable operator families, extraction pipelines | Us (building) |

Barandes lives on Levels 3-5. We're formalizing Levels 1-2. The project operates one level above Barandes: he asks "how do we choose the right representation of physical reality?" — we ask "how do we choose the right representation of *anything*?"

### The 6-Pass Extraction Pipeline

```text
PASS 0: Historical Contingency — Why didn't this appear fifty years earlier?
        (Extracts hidden assumptions of the discipline, not just the theory)
PASS A: Knowledge Extraction — What did they say?
PASS B: Structural Extraction — How is the theory organized?
PASS C: Cognitive Extraction — How did they think?
PASS D: Transfer Extraction — Where else would this operator work?
PASS E: EOS Extraction — What would they contribute to an Epistemic Operating System?
```

PASS 0 is critical — the blind-spot answer (Q34) confirmed that ISP was invisible because of historical contingency, inherited pedagogy, false dichotomies, anthropocentric framing, and representation lock-in. Asking "why was this invisible?" extracts the hidden assumptions of the discipline itself.

### CFA Claude's ISP Identification

CFA Claude independently identified that the ARMADA lever calibration project is empirically estimating the sparse conditional probability matrix of a cognitive ISP. This reframes the entire CFA project:

- Lever YAMLs store P(coherence_impact | worldview_A debating worldview_B) = ISP Axiom 2
- Trinity runs create the classical correlation (via crux declaration) that makes conditional probabilities epistemically accessible = division events
- 10+ golden runs per pairing is justified by ISP: you need enough division events to estimate the conditional distribution reliably

Key testable prediction (Q41): If lever values are ISP sparse conditional probabilities, they should be pair-dependent (CT×PT ≠ CT×G). If they're stable across opponents, something else is going on.

### Q41 Confirmation: Pair-Dependency is Ontological

Q41 confirmed: "Conditional probabilities — and therefore the laws of nature themselves — are fundamentally relational and pair-dependent." Per-matchup YAML architecture is ontologically mandated — not a design convenience.

**IP ≅ Axiom 1 (system-level, predicted pair-invariant):** Identity Preservation maps to ISP's primitive axiom — the one thing that should remain stable regardless of opponent. **Testable immediately** with existing YAML data: compute IP score variance across opponents for each worldview. Low variance → system-intrinsic. High variance → pair-dependent (would require revising the Axiom 1 analogy).

**Lever values ≅ Axiom 2 (pair-dependent, predicted to vary across opponents):** Lever YAMLs store P(coherence_impact | worldview_A debating worldview_B) — a sparse conditional probability. ISP predicts systematic differences across opponents.

**10-golden-run minimum justified by ISP:** You need enough division events to estimate the conditional distribution reliably. Barandes: heavily interacting systems have rapid division events, enabling conditioning "basically anytime you want."

### Nova vs CFA Claude Tension (RESOLVED)

Correlation-creation (CFA Claude) and representation translation (Nova) are successive stages of one pipeline, not competing framings:

```text
Interaction (CFA Claude's level — creates classical correlation)
  → conditional structure becomes accessible
  → comparison becomes possible
  → representation translation (Nova's level — strip vocabulary, find operators)
  → architectural invariants emerge
```

Correlation-creation is the MECHANISM. Translation is the OUTPUT. Both are right; neither is the whole picture.
