## Key Themes (Post-Mining)

### 1. Many-Worlds Demolition (Q1, Q2, Q9, Q12, Q14)
Barandes' isomorphism argument (Hilbert space = harmonic oscillators, Strocchi 1960s / Heslot 1980s) undermines branch-counting. Adlam's self-location paper shows probability is ill-defined without a selection process. Combined: both ontology AND epistemology of Many-Worlds fail simultaneously. Many-Minds "fix" (Albert & Loewer) destroys elegance and renders multiverse redundant — collapses to Barandes' own indivisible stochastic approach.

### 2. The Under-Determination Architecture (Q3, Q16, Q17)
**The central finding.** Adlam's betting argument: "as soon as you specify your goal, the credences are already fixed." NotebookLM found FOUR instances of this pattern (clone betting, coin flip contrast, teleporter identity, and our own RESEARCH_QUESTION.md notes — meta-recursion). The bright line between pure and superficial self-location is ABSOLUTE, not gradual (Barandes: "there IS a bright line," explicitly contrasts with sorites problems). No boundary cases exist.

### 3. Representation Bias Detection (Q1, Q18)
SIX instances of representation-dependent claims disguised as ontological ones. General 3-step detection pipeline extracted:
1. **Isomorphic translation** — rewrite in equivalent representation, see if the "fact" survives
2. **Jaynes omelette check** — audit for macroscopic assumptions baked into microphysics
3. **Refuse evolutionary intuition** — distrust emotional/intuitive claims unless formalizable

### 4. Smuggled Observer Detection (Q9, Q20)
FIVE instances of theories requiring implicit observers they officially deny. Detection pipeline:
1. **Identify explanatory gap** — where does formalism try to cross to lived reality?
2. **Audit selection mechanism** — what exactly is doing the picking/constraining?
3. **Expose missing physics** — if no equation governs the selector, it's a smuggled human artifact

### 5. Personal Identity and the Explanatory Gap (Q6, Q13, Q19)
Adlam (eliminativist) vs Barandes (epistemic humility). The exact boundary is the Explanatory Gap — transition from 3rd-person physical description to 1st-person conscious experience. Adlam: undefinable intuition = psychological illusion. Barandes: undefinable intuition = undeniable reality; if physics can't explain it, the FACTS are insufficient. The "delayed destruction" thought experiment is the sharpest pressure test.

### 6. Consciousness-Agency-Time Triangle (Q5, Q13)
Adlam: if consciousness were passive, no reason it couldn't supervene on multiple places/times. Confinement to one time-slice implies consciousness is fundamentally agential. Explicitly flagged as speculative ("not a very well formed view"). Fictional illustrations: Dr. Manhattan, Slaughterhouse-Five, Arrival, Watchmen.

### 7. Free Will Asymmetry (Q7, Q11)
Free will's undefinability = logical self-cancellation (two conflicting requirements that destroy each other when satisfied). Consciousness's undefinability = epistemic mystery (non-contradictory). Barandes explicitly claims to rescue compatibilism via time-symmetry argument, but stops short of a complete theory.

### 8. Emergence Circularity / Jaynes Omelette (Q8, Q15)
Extended beyond QM to all science. Methodology: inject philosophy into inception phase of physics research. Holography cited as successful model of this collaboration.

### 9. Philosophy-Physics ROI (Q10)
Complete list extracted with dates. Highlights: Bell worked "in secret" at CERN, Deeks (philosopher) independently discovered no-cloning theorem, Deutsch's quantum computing was philosophically motivated. Barandes: "divide output by input — division by zero error."
