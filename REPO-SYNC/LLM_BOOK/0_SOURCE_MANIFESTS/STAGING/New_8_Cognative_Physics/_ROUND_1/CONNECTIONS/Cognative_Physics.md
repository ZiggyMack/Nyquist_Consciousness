# Connections: Cognitive Physics (Adlam & Barandes)

## 1. Hoffman's Consciousness-First Framework

**Strong resonance with Adlam's emergence circularity argument.** Hoffman claims spacetime is a "headset" -- an interface, not fundamental reality. Adlam argues (from within physics) that microphysical theories encode observer perspectives, making emergence claims potentially circular. These converge: if microphysical theories presuppose the macroscopic observer, then perhaps the observer (consciousness) is more fundamental than the microphysics that supposedly produces it.

**Barandes' "recording needle"** maps directly to Hoffman's observer dynamics. Both frame consciousness as irreducible to the physical description -- Hoffman ontologically (consciousness IS reality), Barandes epistemologically (we don't understand enough to reduce it).

**Key tension:** Adlam is more eliminativist than Hoffman. She does not elevate consciousness to fundamental status; she merely notes the circularity. This creates a productive triangulation: Hoffman (consciousness is fundamental) vs. Adlam (we cannot cleanly separate observer from theory) vs. standard physicalism (consciousness emerges from microphysics).

## 2. Frame Theory (Cognitive Architecture, Identity Persistence)

**Adlam's eliminativism about personal identity** is the precise adversarial position that Frame Theory must withstand. She claims there is no "further fact" beyond physical/causal relations -- identity is a pragmatic label, not an ontological category. Frame Theory posits identity as a structural invariant (the "frame" that persists through cognitive transformations).

**Critical connection:** Adlam's "pure vs. superficial self-locating uncertainty" maps onto Frame Theory's distinction between identity-as-label (superficial) and identity-as-structure (deep). Frame Theory would argue that the "goal" that dictates self-locating credences IS the frame -- it is not arbitrary but reflects the cognitive architecture of the agent.

**Barandes' refusal of the teleporter** aligns with Frame Theory: something non-physical (or at least not captured by the physical description) constitutes identity. His epistemic humility ("I don't understand the recording needle") is compatible with Frame Theory's claim that identity persistence is real but not yet formally captured.

## 3. Lucien / Identity Manifold (Persistence, Curvature Accumulation)

**Direct parallel:** Barandes' "amusement park ride" metaphor for consciousness -- traveling along a worldline groove, forming memories, experiencing an illusion of choice. The identity manifold formalizes this: curvature accumulates along the worldline as experiences shape the identity structure.

**Adlam's agential consciousness** provides a constraint for the manifold: the manifold must be experienced one slice at a time (temporal localization) because agency requires it. This suggests the manifold has a *foliation* -- a preferred slicing into time-ordered leaves -- imposed not by physics but by the requirements of consciousness-as-agency.

**Teleporter problem as manifold discontinuity:** Barandes' objection maps to a discontinuity in the identity manifold. The teleporter creates a new manifold point that is causally connected but potentially not *experientially continuous* with the original. Adlam's response: there is no continuity requirement; the manifold is just a convenient description.

## 4. CFA Experiment (Identity Stability Under Adversarial Pressure)

**Adlam's position IS the adversarial attack in CFA.** Her argument -- "there is no fact of the matter about whether you are you" -- is precisely the kind of philosophical pressure that the CFA experiment tests LLM responses against. An LLM confronted with Adlam's eliminativism must either:
- Defend identity persistence (Frame Theory response)
- Adopt eliminativism (Adlam response)
- Find a middle path (Barandes' epistemic humility)

**Self-location credences and CFA:** Adlam shows that in branching/cloning scenarios, "what you care about" immediately dictates credences. In CFA, the question becomes: does the LLM's identity-stance reflect a structural commitment (frame) or an arbitrary goal-choice (Adlam's view)? If the LLM flips identity-stance easily, Adlam wins. If it resists, Frame Theory wins.

## 7. DBEP Framework (Under-Determination Layers)

**The under-determination pattern maps to DBEP layers.** When two parties disagree, the disagreement can originate at Definitions, Beliefs, Expectations, or Perceptions. Adlam's argument shows that under-determination at the Definitions layer (what counts as "identity"? what counts as "existence"?) propagates deterministically through all downstream layers. This is exactly why CFA's Phase 1 (Definitions) must be resolved before Phase 2 (Beliefs/Expectations about lever values) — otherwise you're scoring within a definition you haven't stabilized.

**Notebook F (DBEP Analysis) from RESEARCH_QUESTION.md** was designed to test this: for every disagreement in the transcript, determine whether it originates in D, B, E, or P. The teleporter disagreement is pure D (what counts as "you"). The Many-Worlds dispute is D+B (what counts as "existence" + whether branches are real). The free will dispute is D (what counts as "freedom").

## 8. EOS (Institutional Design, Philosophy-Physics Collaboration)
