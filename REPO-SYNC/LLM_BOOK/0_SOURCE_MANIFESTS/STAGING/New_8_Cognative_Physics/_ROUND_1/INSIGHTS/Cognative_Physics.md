# Insights: Cognitive Physics (Adlam & Barandes)

## Novel Arguments and Contrarian Positions

### 1. The Isomorphism Attack on Many-Worlds (Barandes)

Barandes presents what appears to be a new argument (he says this is the first time he has presented it publicly): the Hilbert space axioms are mathematically isomorphic to a system of classical harmonic oscillators. In the oscillator picture, a zero-amplitude branch corresponds to a spring at rest -- which is still *a spring*. It has not ceased to exist. Therefore the Everettian claim that zero-amplitude branches "vanish" is representation-dependent and cannot be justified from the mathematical axioms alone. This undermines a foundational assumption of the many-worlds framework.

**Why this matters:** If branches at zero amplitude persist, the ontology of Everettian QM becomes far stranger than advertised. The "elegant" picture (just the wavefunction, nothing else) is revealed to depend on a privileged mathematical representation.

### 2. Self-Location as Goal-Expression, Not Belief (Adlam)

Adlam's key insight: in pure self-locating uncertainty (cloning scenarios, Everettian branches), credences are not beliefs about the world. They are expressions of practical goals. Wanting to maximize total winnings across all copies yields equal credences; wanting a specific copy to win yields probability-one for that copy. There is no intermediate epistemic step. This collapses the entire self-location probability literature.

**Contrarian impact:** This attacks the Doomsday Argument, the Simulation Argument, Boltzmann brain reasoning, and anthropic reasoning in cosmology -- all of which presuppose rational constraints on self-locating credences.

### 3. Agency as Explanation for Temporal Localization of Consciousness (Adlam)

Why do we experience one time-slice rather than our entire worldline? Adlam's answer: because consciousness is fundamentally agential, not passive. If you were conscious of multiple times simultaneously, causal paradoxes would arise (acting at time T based on knowledge of time T+1). Agency *requires* temporal localization. This is not merely a constraint on information -- it is a structural requirement of being an agent.

**Novel connection:** This inverts the standard physicalist story. Rather than consciousness being explained by brain-states-at-a-time, the *temporal structure of brain states* is explained by the requirements of agency.

### 4. The "Recording Needle" and Epistemic Humility (Barandes)

Barandes frames conscious experience as a "recording needle" that landed on one particular body -- the single most important event that ever happened to him, and one he does not understand at all. This grounds his refusal of the teleporter: without understanding how the needle works, you cannot trust the teleporter to preserve it. This is an *epistemological* argument against Adlam's eliminativism, not an ontological one.

### 5. Emergence Circularity (Adlam)

Our microphysical theories were derived using macroscopic instruments and encode macroscopic observer-perspectives. Attempting to "derive" the macroscopic world from these theories may be circular. Adlam cites Jaynes: "the inferential stuff and the causal stuff are scrambled up in a big omelette that no one can unscramble." This challenges the standard reductionist program from within.

### 6. Indivisible Stochastic Causation (Barandes)

Barandes' approach provides directed conditional probabilities at the microphysical level that ground causation without privileging a time direction. The arrow of time is contingent (initial conditions), not fundamental (laws). This threads the needle between eliminativism about micro-causation and the manifest directedness of macro-causation.
