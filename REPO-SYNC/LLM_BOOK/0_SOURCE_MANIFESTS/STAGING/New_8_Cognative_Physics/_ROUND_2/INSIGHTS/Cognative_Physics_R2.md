**H:** CFA deliberation data will show non-Markovian structure — prediction accuracy from arbitrary turns < prediction accuracy from crux turns (Experiment 1).

**O:** Stochastic-Quantum Correspondence maps vintage probabilities losslessly to Hilbert space axioms. ### 3. Measurement Problem Resolution — No Superposition Required

**O:** "There are no literal superpositions in this picture"; "there's no fundamental wave function"; "observers are just regular systems." Division events replace measurement axioms. **E:** Reconstructs what the standard axioms actually claim before critiquing them. The Dirac-von Neumann axioms are faithfully laid out, their singularity identified (ambiguity for big systems), then replaced rather than patched. Candidate: OP-004 (Reconstruction Before Judgment).

**T:** CFA deliberation ≅ ISP system-environment interaction, via the mechanism of adversarial exchange creating classical correlation that produces emergent conditioning times (cruxes). The crux replaces any need for arbitrary "measurement" moments in deliberation analysis.

**H:** Cruxes emerge from interaction density, not from arbitrary protocol design. Isolated evaluation produces fewer cruxes than adversarial exchange. Buddhism's zero-crux result predicted by ISP: insufficient interaction density.

**Alternative Interpretation:** Representation-invariance may be necessary but not sufficient for ontological candidacy — a feature could survive every representation and still be an artifact of the interaction protocol rather than a property of the system.

### 15. Cost of Representation Change — When Is "Simpler" Better?

**O:** Hilbert space computes best (mature mathematical tools, "epistemic management of configuration data"). ISP explains and teaches best (clear physical picture, extensible "knobs you can turn"). **T:** CFA faces the same tradeoff — 1-10 scoring is computationally tractable but may hide structure. Need both representations available for different purposes (analysis vs prediction vs explanation).
