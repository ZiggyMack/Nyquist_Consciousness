### Prediction 1: Non-Markov Signature
**Claim:** Conditioning on arbitrary turns predicts WORSE than conditioning on crux turns.
**Falsification:** If arbitrary-turn and crux-turn conditioning yield equal prediction accuracy, the process is Markovian and ISP model fails.
**Maps to:** Experiment 1

### Prediction 2: Crux Frequency ∝ Interaction Density
**Claim:** Isolation → near-zero cruxes. Standard Trinity → moderate crux frequency. High-density → spike, then smoothing to quasi-continuous.
**Falsification:** If crux frequency is constant across interaction levels, cruxes are protocol artifacts, not interaction-emergent.
**Maps to:** Experiment 9 (3 explicit density levels designed)

### Prediction 3: Inter-Crux Interpolation Fails
**Claim:** Dense models of turn-by-turn inter-crux evolution will overfit/fail on out-of-sample data. Probing mid-thought creates new cruxes rather than revealing existing state.
**Falsification:** If dense inter-crux models outperform sparse crux-conditioned models on out-of-sample data, the ISP epistemic barrier doesn't apply to cognition.
**Maps to:** Experiment 3

### Prediction 4: No Superposition Effects
**Claim:** Identity scores always reflect a definite state (is-to-is updates). No interference patterns, no bimodal "superposition" distributions at crux points.
**Falsification:** If crux-point score distributions show bimodality or interference-like patterns, the agent's state may not be definite between measurements.
**Maps to:** Experiment 3
