## Why it's here

Category Theory shares CA's obsession with invariants and transformations. Several mappings have been noted between CA constructs and categorical language:

| CA Construct | Categorical Interpretation | Strength |
|-------------|---------------------------|----------|
| Operators | Morphisms (transformations between reasoning states) | Suggestive — operators do have domains/codomains |
| CRUX declaration | Non-existence of structure-preserving morphism | Precise — stronger than "they disagree" |
| Coupling Probe | Functor-like mapping test | Loose — needs formalization |
| Multi-extractor convergence | Natural transformation | Suggestive — extractor-independence is naturality |
| Universal operators | Universal constructions | Speculative — requires much more data |

These are observations, not evidence. They become meaningful only if empirical data independently confirms the structures they predict.

## What would promote it

- At least one prediction confirmed above chance
- Demonstrated compression: fewer categorical concepts explain more operator relationships than the current graph
- Novel prediction: the categorical framing predicts something the operator graph alone does not

## What would retire it

- Both predictions fail (composition is random, score profiles don't compose)
- A simpler framework (graph theory, plain algebra) achieves equal or better compression
- The categorical vocabulary adds jargon without adding explanatory power
