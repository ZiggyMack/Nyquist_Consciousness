## Why Hermann?

Grete Hermann is historically validated: she found a fatal flaw in von Neumann's 1932 "proof" that hidden variables are impossible — **decades before Bell** (1966). The physics community accepted von Neumann's proof as definitive for over 30 years. Hermann's 1935 paper identified the circular reasoning but was largely ignored. This makes her a perfect test case for the "Philosophical Auditing" operator: systematic detection of logical errors in consensus work that the field's own practitioners cannot see.

She also provides the **Noether lineage test** — as Noether's student, her cognitive architecture should exhibit Noetherian characteristics. If the Noether Lens (PASS C) finds strong invariance-seeking in Hermann, that's evidence the lens captures real cognitive inheritance, not just a pattern we project.

## Recommended Sources (Priority Order)

### Tier 1: ESSENTIAL

**1. "The Foundations of Quantum Mechanics in the Philosophy of Nature" (1935)**
- Original German: "Die naturphilosophischen Grundlagen der Quantenmechanik"
- Published in Abhandlungen der Fries'schen Schule, Neue Folge, Band 6
- Contains the von Neumann critique that was ignored for 30 years
- English translation by Dirk Lumma available in "The Harvard Review of Philosophy" (1999)
- Also translated in full by Elise Crull and Guido Bacciagaluppi

**2. Elise Crull & Guido Bacciagaluppi, "Grete Hermann: Between Physics and Philosophy" (2017)**
- Springer — most comprehensive English-language resource
- Contains translations of Hermann's key works PLUS scholarly commentary
- Commentary reveals Hermann's methodology and intellectual context
- This is the closest thing to a "biography of her thinking"

**3. Hermann's correspondence with Heisenberg and Weizsacker**
- Some published in the Crull/Bacciagaluppi volume
- Reveals her process: how she argued, what she noticed, how she pushed back
- Conversational format — closer to interview-style process revelation

### Tier 2: HIGH VALUE

**4. Hermann's essays on Neo-Kantian philosophy of science**
- Her philosophical framework: transcendental idealism applied to physics
- Reveals the LENS through which she found the von Neumann error
- Key question: was the error visible BECAUSE of philosophical training that physicists lacked?

**5. Academic papers analyzing Hermann's contribution**
- Jeffrey Bub, "Von Neumann's 'No Hidden Variables' Proof: A Re-Appraisal" (2010)
- Various papers in the 2010s reassessing Hermann's discovery
- These provide the historical context for PASS 0

**6. Material on the Fries-Nelson school**
- Hermann worked within the Leonard Nelson neo-Kantian tradition
- Understanding this school reveals the philosophical tools she used
- Was her error-detection a product of specific training or general philosophical rigor?

### Tier 3: BACKGROUND

**7. Von Neumann's original proof (1932)**
- "Mathematical Foundations of Quantum Mechanics" Chapter IV
- Needed to understand exactly WHAT Hermann found wrong
- The circular assumption: linearity of expectation values assumed for hidden variables

**8. Bell's 1966 paper**
- "On the Problem of Hidden Variables in Quantum Mechanics"
- Bell independently rediscovered Hermann's objection
- Comparison: did Bell use the SAME cognitive operation as Hermann, or a different one?
