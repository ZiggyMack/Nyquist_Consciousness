# Research Question: Hestenes' Cognitive Architecture

## Core Hypothesis

David Hestenes is the **anti-Dirac**. Where Dirac generated physics via geometry and then *suppressed* the geometry to publish austere algebra, Hestenes' entire program is to **reverse-engineer suppressed structure back out** — showing (for example) that the "imaginary unit" *i* in the Dirac equation is really a geometric object (a bivector = the plane of electron spin), and that standard matrix formalism carries "16 redundant degrees of freedom" that obscure a literal spacetime geometry.

If true, Hestenes is the ideal test of the protocol's new **Tacit-Heuristic Probe** (v1.3): he *verbalizes and formalizes the recovery* of exactly the kind of hidden model that Dirac refused to explain (the "Penrose Silence"). The dig either confirms the probe against a cooperative subject or exposes where even an explicit geometer's method resists decomposition.

## Specific Question Clusters

### 1. Process of recovery (not results)
- How does Hestenes *find* what a formalism hides? What is his repeatable method for detecting that an algebraic object secretly encodes a geometric one?
- Does he work backward from a redundancy/anomaly in the algebra to a geometric cause, or forward from geometry to re-derive the algebra?

### 2. Candidate operator — Geometric De-obfuscation
- Hestenes repeatedly translates an *algebraic redundancy* into a *literal geometric reality* to eliminate "superfluous degrees of freedom." Is this a repeatable cognitive move (an "operator")? What is its detection condition and its failure mode?

### 3. Candidate operator — Scaffolding Extraction (the Tacit-Heuristic Probe, as a thinker's own move)
- Identifying what a prior thinker *hid* to make their equations work. Hestenes does this *to Dirac*. Does he have a systematic method for reconstructing a suppressed generative model from a finished formalism? (This is precisely our v1.3 Tacit-Heuristic Probe — does Hestenes formalize it better than we do?)

### 4. Relationship to Dirac (context inlined)
- *Context: "Dirac" is the physicist P.A.M. Dirac, subject of our prior dig. He generated ideas with projective geometry but deliberately suppressed it and published austere algebra; when asked to explain the geometry→physics link he refused ("a shake of the head").* Given this, is Hestenes' method the exact **inverse** of Dirac's — recovery vs suppression? What does Hestenes claim Dirac's algebra *cost* in understanding?

### 5. Museum rediscoveries (operators defined inline; answer from the sources)
- **Representation ≠ Ontology** (separate what a formalism *says* from what actually *exists*): does Hestenes exhibit it? (His thesis looks like a pure case — the matrix representation hid the geometric ontology.)
- **Subtractive Discovery** (make progress by *removing* an assumption/entity, not adding one): does removing the complex scalar field count?
- **Under-Determination Detection** (notice when a formalism does not uniquely fix an outcome): does he flag places where the standard interpretation over-claims?

### 6. The "Nerfed Equation" test (cross-project)
- Hestenes admits his real-algebra formulation is *mathematically isomorphic* to the standard one (same predictions). So: was anything genuinely **lost**, or only **renotated**? If structure was obscured but not lost, what is the difference, operationally? What does his "spin was inadvertently smuggled in" claim imply about representation invariance?

### 7. Negative control — where does the protocol struggle?
- Dirac was tacit and resisted extraction. Hestenes is explicit and pedagogical. Does the protocol therefore *over*-extract him (finding structure because he hands it over)? Where does his reasoning still resist operator decomposition?
