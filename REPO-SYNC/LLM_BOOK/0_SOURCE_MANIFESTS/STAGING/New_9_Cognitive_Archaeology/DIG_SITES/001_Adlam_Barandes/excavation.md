## Recovered Operators

### OP-001: Representation ≠ Ontology

**Definition:** Separate what the formalism says from what actually exists — a mathematical description that is isomorphic to another cannot claim ontological priority over it.

**Example from source:** Barandes demonstrates that Hilbert space axioms are mathematically isomorphic to a system of classical harmonic oscillators. In the oscillator picture, a zero-amplitude branch corresponds to a spring at rest — which is still a spring. The Everettian claim that zero-amplitude branches "vanish" depends on a privileged mathematical representation, not on the physics. (Q1, Q18)

**Also seen in:** CFA (FUT's representation→evaluation flow), care package Finding 2 (twin detection pipelines)

**Novel or rediscovery?** NEW — registered as Museum OP-001. Extremely likely to appear in Pearl (causal graphs vs physical systems), Lakoff (conceptual metaphor vs literal truth), and Dennett (intentional stance vs physical description).

### OP-002: Hidden Selection Audit

**Definition:** When an outcome appears determined, ask: what mechanism selected it? If no mechanism can be identified, something has been smuggled in.

**Example from source:** Barandes on consciousness: the "recording needle" landed on one particular body — the single most important event in his existence, and one he doesn't understand. What selected this body? If no physics governs the selection, the apparent determinacy is a human artifact. (Q4, Q20)

**Also seen in:** NotebookLM independently generated "Ask what is doing the selecting" as a recurring pattern across the entire transcript.

**Novel or rediscovery?** NEW — registered as Museum OP-002.

### OP-003: Goal → Optimization Collapse

**Definition:** In certain domains, specifying your goal immediately and completely determines your rational strategy — no intermediate empirical or theoretical steps exist.

**Example from source:** Adlam: in pure self-locating uncertainty (cloning scenarios), "as soon as you specify your goal, the credences are already fixed." Wanting to maximize total winnings across all copies yields equal credences; wanting a specific copy to win yields probability-one. The empirical gap vanishes entirely. (Q3, Q16)

**Also seen in:** Master→DBEP framework (Master/Telos determines Description→Belief→Evaluation→Prediction stack). NotebookLM independently found RESEARCH_QUESTION.md as a 4th instance of this pattern (Q16).

**Novel or rediscovery?** NEW — registered as Museum OP-003.

### OP-004: Reconstruction Before Judgment

**Definition:** Faithfully reconstruct the object of study in its own terms before evaluating it from an external framework.

**Example from source:** Barandes insists on translating claims into equivalent mathematical representations before assessing them. Adlam reconstructs the self-location puzzle in decision-theoretic terms before declaring credences meaningless. Both refuse to evaluate before reconstruction is complete. (Throughout transcript)

**Also seen in:** CFA Phase sequence (Phase 1a: faithful reconstruction, Phase 1b: internal coherence, then Phase 2: lever calibration). FUT's qualification procedure. EOS institutional analysis.

**Novel or rediscovery?** NEW — registered as Museum OP-004. This is the operator that CFA, FUT, and EOS all share. Strong candidate for early GREEN status.

### OP-005: Hidden Structure Injection

**Definition:** Detect when an analysis quietly imports evaluators, observers, coordinate systems, representations, or optimization targets without declaring them.

**Example from source:** Two parallel detection methods that Nova collapsed into one:

These are duals: representation bias injects structure through the description; smuggled observer injects structure through the describer. Same operation, different entry point.

**Novel or rediscovery?** NEW — registered as Museum OP-005. Nova's unification (2026-07-05).

### OP-006: Under-Determination Detection

**Definition:** When a formalism underdetermines an outcome, any procedure that claims to determine one is importing extra structure. Identify the import.

**Example from source:** Nova's synthesis: the common abstraction between Adlam (quantum credences), Barandes (identity persistence), CFA (Phase 1 scoring without anchors), and emergence (macro from micro) is under-determination. In each case, the formalism doesn't determine the answer — something external must be imported to resolve it. (Nova, 2026-07-05)

## Recovered Failure Modes

### FM-1: Representation Reification

**Related operator:** OP-001 (Representation ≠ Ontology)
**How it manifests:** Treating a mathematically convenient description as physically real. Claiming branches "vanish" because amplitudes go to zero in one representation, when they persist in an isomorphic one.
**Example from source:** Many-worlds interpretation treating Hilbert space as ontologically privileged.

### FM-2: Emergence Circularity

**Related operator:** OP-005 (Hidden Structure Injection)
**How it manifests:** Claiming to derive macroscopic reality from microphysics, when the microphysical theories were built using macroscopic instruments and encode macroscopic observer perspectives. "The inferential stuff and the causal stuff are scrambled up in a big omelette." (Jaynes via Adlam)
**Example from source:** Adlam's emergence circularity argument — the bottom-up derivation is circular because the bottom was built from the top.

### FM-3: Premature Evaluation

**Related operator:** OP-004 (Reconstruction Before Judgment)
**How it manifests:** Evaluating a framework using categories it doesn't recognize. Scoring "moral substance" using a definition the framework doesn't accept.
**Example from source:** CRUX_MS in CFA — auditors defining "Moral Substance" differently, causing crux declarations that are definitional disagreements, not philosophical ones.

### FM-4: Selection Blindness

**Related operator:** OP-002 (Hidden Selection Audit)
**How it manifests:** Accepting an outcome as "natural" or "obvious" without identifying the selection mechanism. Things don't just happen — something picks them.
**Example from source:** Born rule in quantum mechanics — outcomes are selected by a mechanism nobody can identify. The rule works, but the selector is invisible.

### A-1: Formalizability Requirement

**Stated or implicit?** Mostly implicit. Both speakers assume that reasoning operations should be expressible in formal terms. Barandes insists on mathematical isomorphism; Adlam insists on decision-theoretic formalization.
**Impact if false:** If some genuine reasoning operations are inherently informal, the excavation methodology would miss them.

**Stated or implicit?** Explicit (Adlam, Q5). Evolved intuitions about identity and probability should not be trusted as evidence for philosophical claims.
**Impact if false:** If evolutionary intuitions track genuine features of reality, dismissing them loses information.
