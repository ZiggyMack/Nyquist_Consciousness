## Recommended Sources (Priority Order)

### Tier 1: ESSENTIAL

**1. "The Book of Why" (2018)**
- Pearl & Dana Mackenzie, Basic Books
- Written for general audience — Pearl describes his THINKING, not just his formalism
- Chapters on the "causal revolution," the ladder of causation, counterfactual reasoning
- Most process-revealing of his works — anecdotes about HOW ideas developed

**2. Long-form interviews and lectures**
- **Lex Fridman Podcast** — Pearl has appeared, long-form conversational
- **Machine Learning Street Talk** — technical but process-focused
- **Various conference keynotes** — Pearl is a good lecturer who reveals methodology
- Look for interviews where he discusses the HISTORY of his thinking, not just the current state

**3. "Causality: Models, Reasoning, and Inference" (2000, 2nd ed. 2009)**
- Cambridge University Press
- The technical monograph — Chapters 1-2 (motivation and methodology) are the key extraction targets
- Preface of the 2nd edition reflects on what changed in his thinking over 9 years
- Dense with formalism but the introductions reveal architecture

### Tier 2: HIGH VALUE

**4. Pearl's blog posts (UCLA)**
- Technical but often polemical — arguments against rival approaches
- Polemics reveal cognitive architecture: what does Pearl consider BAD reasoning?
- His arguments against "Big Data" approaches are particularly operator-rich

**5. "Causal Inference in Statistics: A Primer" (2016)**
- Pearl, Glymour, Jewell — Cambridge University Press
- Textbook — the pedagogical choices reveal what Pearl considers essential vs dispensable
- Tests Pedagogical Forcing (OP-012): does teaching the material change what Pearl emphasizes?

**6. Turing Award Lecture (2012)**
- "The Mechanization of Causal Reasoning"
- ~1 hour — distilled career summary
- What does Pearl CHOOSE to highlight about his own intellectual trajectory?

### Tier 3: BACKGROUND

**7. Pearl's early work on Bayesian networks (1980s)**
- "Probabilistic Reasoning in Intelligent Systems" (1988)
- Shows the TRANSITION from Bayesian networks to causal reasoning
- The cognitive architecture of the transition may be more revealing than either endpoint

**8. Debates with statisticians (Rubin, Dawid)**
- Pearl vs the potential outcomes framework
- Adversarial context reveals which operators Pearl deploys under pressure
- Structural analog to CFA deliberation
