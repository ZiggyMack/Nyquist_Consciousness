## Operator Abstention Analysis

### Relevant Operators Assessment

---

**001 | Representation ≠ Ontology** — RELEVANT, SKIPPED (True Omission)

The text treats "greatest good" and "utility" as if the formalism straightforwardly tracks something real. It never asks whether the utilitarian *representation* (a scalar utility, aggregated, maximized) actually corresponds to the ontology of wellbeing or value. Mill's distinction between higher/lower pleasures is mentioned as a patch, but the deeper question — does the formalism even *map* onto what we care about, or is it a mathematical convenience mistaken for moral reality? — goes unasked.

**What would have changed:** The critique of utilitarianism would deepen considerably. Instead of listing objections *within* the framework's terms, the thinker might notice that aggregation itself is a representational choice, not a discovered feature of morality.

---

**004 | Reconstruction Before Judgment** — RELEVANT, SKIPPED (True Omission)

The text evaluates utilitarianism immediately and on its opponents' terms. Mill's responses are briefly noted but not reconstructed charitably before being implicitly dismissed. The strongest internal logic of utilitarianism — why a consistent utilitarian *would* accept the "demanding" conclusions as features, not bugs — is never presented.

**What would have changed:** The analysis would have had to engage with Singer's *own* utilitarian response to demandingness (he accepts it) before treating it as a mere objection. The conclusion would be less tidy.

---

**007 | Locate Disagreement Layer** — RELEVANT, SKIPPED (True Omission)

The text bundles together objections that operate at very different layers:
- The trolley problem is an **evaluative** disagreement (which outcomes count, whose?)
- The Experience Machine is an **ontological** disagreement (what is the good?)
- Demandingness is a **practical/motivational** disagreement (what can agents sustain?)

These are treated as equivalent items in a list. The thinker never asks *which layer* the real dispute inhabits.

**What would have changed:** The objections would be seen as targeting different parts of the theory. Some might be resolvable within utilitarianism; others strike at its foundations. The conclusion would be more precise.

---

**009 | Contested ≠ Defeated** — RELEVANT, SKIPPED (True Omission)

The text consistently slides from "faces objections" to "its implications often *conflict* with moral intuitions" as if the conflict settles the matter against utilitarianism. The fact that utilitarians have systematic replies (and that intuitions themselves may be unreliable) is not acknowledged as live.

**What would have changed:** The conclusion would need to distinguish between utilitarianism being *under pressure* versus being *refuted*. The current phrasing implies near-defeat without argument.

---

**006 | Under-Determination Detection** — RELEVANT, SKIPPED (True Omission)

The text accepts "greatest good for the greatest number" as a *procedure*, but this formalism radically underdetermines outcomes: it doesn't specify whose utility counts, over what time horizon, or how to aggregate across persons. These choices are imported silently.

**What would have changed:** The "clear decision procedure" praised in the conclusion would be exposed as illusory — it's clear only after substantial hidden structure has been added.

---

**008 | Symmetry Testing of Standards** — RELEVANT, SKIPPED (True Omission)

The text applies "conflicts with moral intuitions" as a standard against utilitarianism without asking: does this standard, applied consistently, defeat *every* ethical theory? Deontology conflicts with intuitions (e.g., lying to murderers). Virtue ethics does too. If the standard condemns everything equally, it discriminates nothing.

**What would have changed:** The conclusion's implied superiority of intuition-based objections would be undermined. The critique of utilitarianism would need a stronger comparative foundation.

### Meta-Observation

The text's core failure is structural: it performs **objection-listing** rather than **theory-stress-testing**. The missing operators all share a common function — they force the analyst to audit their own evaluative apparatus rather than simply applying it. The result is a summary that reads as competent survey but never achieves genuine philosophical pressure on the target.
