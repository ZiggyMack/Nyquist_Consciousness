# Dig Site 000: Pre-Registration

> **Future researchers should be able to read this document and know what we believed before the data existed.**

This document records expectations, thresholds, and falsification conditions for Dig Site 000 **before** any data is observed. Its purpose is to prevent unconscious goalpost movement and preserve the epistemic integrity of Cognitive Archaeology.

**No thresholds below may be modified after the experiment begins.**

---

## Constitutional Principle

> Dig Site 000 is not designed to validate Cognitive Archaeology. It is designed to validate whether Cognitive Archaeology's first instrument deserves to measure anything at all.

---

## 1. Primary Research Question

Can reasoning operators be recovered in a manner that is substantially independent of the extractor?

## 2. Primary Hypothesis

There exists a non-trivial level of extractor-independent convergence in reasoning operator identification.

## 3. Null Hypothesis

Observed convergence is primarily explained by the extractor rather than the thinker.

> The recovered operators are better explained as artifacts of the extraction pipeline than as recurring reasoning structures within the source material.

---

## 4. Success Criteria (Frozen)

| Criterion | Threshold | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Extractor agreement | Average pairwise agreement ≥ 40% | Below this, extractor variance dominates |
| Negative controls | Measurably fewer and less specific operators than genuine reasoning | If indistinguishable, extraction generates rather than detects |
| Granularity stability | Operators recognizable across ≥ 2 description grains | If grain-dependent, operators are prompt artifacts |
| Blind extraction | ≥ 3 extractors agree on ≥ 50% of operators from same source | Rules out single-extractor tic |

## 5. Failure Criteria (Frozen)

Dig Site 000 is considered **unsuccessful** if ANY of the following occur:

- Extractor agreement falls below 30% pairwise
- Negative controls produce operator structures indistinguishable from genuine reasoning
- Convergence disappears under small prompt wording changes
- Operators prove highly extractor-specific (each extractor finds a non-overlapping set)

**"Unsuccessful" does not mean the project is dead. It means instrument redesign is required before Dig Site 002.** That distinction matters enormously.

---

## 6. What Are We Actually Testing?

We are NOT testing whether Cognitive Archaeology is true.

We are testing whether **our current measurement instrument deserves to proceed.**

This protects the project: a failed Dig Site 000 kills the current extraction protocol, not the research question.

## 8. What Would Surprise Us?

Surprises become theory generators.

### I would be surprised if...

- ...every extractor recovered nearly identical operators. (Would suggest operators are more "obvious" than we assumed — which would be a strong positive result.)

- ...negative controls generated rich operator structures. (Would mean extraction is fundamentally broken — or that reasoning operators are far more ubiquitous than hypothesized.)

- ...human coders disagreed more than LLMs. (Would suggest LLM convergence is an artifact of shared training rather than genuine detection.)

- ...OP-006 survived while OP-001 failed. (Would invert our confidence hierarchy completely.)

- ...granularity sensitivity was the failure mode, not extractor disagreement. (Would mean the operators are real but our decomposition grain is arbitrary.)

## 10. Permitted Conclusions

Before seeing the data, we define what conclusions are allowed.

### PASS

All four success criteria met.

→ Proceed to Dig Site 002. Current extraction protocol is validated as an instrument. Existing Museum operators retain their confidence levels.

### PARTIAL PASS

Some criteria met, others marginal. No failure criteria triggered.

→ Revise extractor protocol for the failing dimensions. Repeat Dig Site 000 with revised protocol before proceeding.

### FAIL

One or more failure criteria triggered.

→ Suspend excavation. Investigate instrument. All current Museum operators downgraded to RED pending methodology revision. No new dig sites until the instrument question is resolved.
