# Catalog of Cognitive Operators

## 1. Exposing problem-relocation in solutions

**Definition:** When an objection proposes a fix, you show that the fix merely moves the original problem to a new level rather than dissolving it. The same cognitive challenge recurs at the meta-level.

**Examples from text:**
- The delegation objection ("voters can delegate to trusted experts") is shown to relocate the evaluation problem: "how does a non-expert evaluate which expert to trust?"
- The structure is recursive — any proposed proxy faces the same evaluative gap that motivated the proxy.

**Failure when absent:** You accept relocated solutions as genuine solutions. A debate stalls because each round of "just defer to X" regenerates the original problem indefinitely without anyone noticing the recursion.

## 2. Identifying unstated preservation assumptions

**Definition:** An argument claims that some property (information, diversity, preference intensity) survives transit through a mechanism. You isolate the unstated premise that the mechanism preserves that property, then test whether it actually does.

**Examples from text:**
- "Cognitive diversity compensates for individual ignorance" is shown to depend on the unstated premise that "the aggregation mechanism preserves the information that diversity generates."
- The preservation premise is the load-bearing one; the diversity claim is idle without it.

**Failure when absent:** You accept mechanism-outputs as faithful carriers of mechanism-inputs. Arguments about aggregation, mediation, or representation pass unchallenged because nobody asks whether the channel preserves the signal.

## 3. Attacking via conditional vulnerability

**Definition:** Rather than flatly denying an opponent's premise, you specify a concrete condition under which the premise fails, then argue the condition is live. The premise is shown to be contingent, not categorical.

**Examples from text:**
- Rather than deny that diversity compensates for ignorance, the text specifies the defeating condition: "if the aggregation mechanism is vulnerable to manipulation (gerrymandering, media capture, algorithmic amplification)."
- The diversity argument doesn't fail in principle; it fails *if* a specific vulnerability is present.

**Failure when absent:** You either accept premises as unconditional or reject them wholesale. You lose the ability to say "this works under conditions C but not under conditions D," collapsing a structured argument into a binary.

## 4. Reframing locus of dependency

**Definition:** You relocate the variable that determines an outcome from one level (individual capacity) to another (structural environment). The explanatory weight shifts from properties of agents to properties of systems.

**Examples from text:**
- "Quality of democratic institutions depends not on the epistemic capacity of individual voters but on the structural integrity of the information environment."
- The prior framing treated voter ignorance as the bottleneck; the new framing treats information-environment integrity as the bottleneck.

**Failure when absent:** Interventions target the wrong layer. You pour resources into fixing individual competence when the system-level property is what governs outcomes, or vice versa.

---

## 5. Deriving structural tension from joint premises

**Definition:** You assemble premises that are individually plausible but jointly incompatible, then show that the incompatibility is architectural — it cannot be resolved without abandoning one of the premises. The conclusion is a tension, not a policy recommendation.

**Examples from text:**
- Premise 1 (legitimacy requires evaluation) + Premise 2 (policy requires expertise voters lack) + Premise 3 (lack of expertise breaks evaluation) jointly produce a structural tension.
- The conclusion is that the tension is built in, not that one premise should be dropped.

**Failure when absent:** You treat systemic incompatibilities as if they were solvable trade-offs. You search for a policy fix when the problem is that the premises cannot all hold simultaneously.
