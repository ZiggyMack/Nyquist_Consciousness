**Catalog of Reasoning Operators**

**1. Distinguishing qualitative subtypes**  
Separates members of a single category (e.g., pleasures, goods) into hierarchically ordered kinds on non-quantitative grounds.  
- Mill’s higher vs. lower pleasures  
- Intellectual vs. bodily experiences  
- Subjective experience vs. other valued goods (Nozick)  
Absence collapses all instances into a single metric, erasing grounds for differential weighting.

**2. Testing via constructed scenario**  
Introduces a minimal hypothetical that isolates one variable to probe whether a stated criterion actually tracks the relevant value.  
- Nozick’s Experience Machine  
- Trolley problem (harm one/save many)  
- Singer’s drowning child  
Without it, evaluation remains at the level of abstract statement and cannot reveal whether the criterion matches considered judgments.

**3. Deriving extreme implications**  
Extrapolates the principle to its logical limit to expose scope or demandingness.  
- Perpetual maximization leaving no room for personal projects  
- Routine sacrifice of the innocent for aggregate gain  
- Requirement to abandon all non-utility-maximizing activity  
Absence leaves the principle’s practical reach unexamined and its boundary conditions untested.

**4. Confronting principle with standing intuitions**  
Places the output of the decision procedure beside pre-existing judgments about rights, justice, or integrity without first revising those judgments.  
- Utilitarian permission to harm one for many vs. rights-based prohibitions  
- Total utility maximization vs. personal integrity constraints  
- Aggregate good vs. justice constraints  
Absence allows the theory to redefine or override the conflicting intuition rather than registering the mismatch.
