**Catalog of Reasoning Operators**

**Identifying hidden assumptions**  
Spots unstated premises required for an argument to hold.  
- Assumes aggregation (voting) preserves diversity-generated information  
- Assumes non-experts can evaluate expert trustworthiness  
- Assumes cognitive diversity directly scales to collective epistemic success  
Absent: Arguments appear self-contained when they actually rest on fragile, unexamined bridges.

**Tracing problem relocation**  
Shows a proposed fix merely moves the original difficulty to a new location without eliminating it.  
- Delegation to experts relocates evaluation from policy to expert selection  
- Expert-selection problem recreates the original voter-ignorance problem  
Absent: Solutions are accepted at face value even though they preserve the core obstacle.

**Distinguishing component vs. system properties**  
Separates attributes of individual agents from attributes of the institutional or informational system they inhabit.  
- Contrasts individual voter ignorance with structural integrity of the information environment  
- Separates diversity’s generative capacity from aggregation mechanism’s preservation capacity  
Absent: Diagnosis remains fixed at the individual level and misses higher-order failure modes.

**Identifying conditional argument failure**  
Specifies an additional condition that must obtain for a claim to succeed and shows the claim collapses when that condition is absent.  
- Diversity argument succeeds only if aggregation is manipulation-resistant  
- Legitimacy claim holds only if voters can track policy quality through expertise barriers  
Absent: Arguments are treated as generally valid rather than conditionally fragile.
