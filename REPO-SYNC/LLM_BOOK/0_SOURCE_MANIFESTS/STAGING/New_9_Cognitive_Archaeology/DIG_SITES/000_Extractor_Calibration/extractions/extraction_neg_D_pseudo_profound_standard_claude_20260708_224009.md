## Catalog of Reasoning Operations

After careful analysis, I must be direct: **this text performs almost no genuine reasoning operations**. It primarily strings together evocative assertions without argumentative structure. However, it does perform several *pseudo-reasoning* operations — moves that resemble reasoning while avoiding its demands. These are worth cataloging precisely because identifying them is a critical reasoning skill.

---

### 1. Asserting Conclusions as Premises

**Definition:** Stating the point to be established as if it were already established, skipping the inferential work entirely.

**Examples:**
- "The boundaries between self and cosmos dissolve into pure awareness" — presented as fact, not argued
- "The boundary between mind and matter is an illusion" — stated without evidence

**What goes wrong when absent:** Arguments become circular or collapse entirely, revealing that no actual support exists.

---

### 2. Laundering Claims Through Prestige Vocabulary

**Definition:** Borrowing terminology from legitimate disciplines to give unargued assertions the appearance of empirical grounding.

**Examples:**
- "Fundamental frequency of existence" borrows from physics without using physics
- "Quantum observer effect" is cited to support a conclusion about consciousness that the actual physics does not support
- "Fabric of spacetime" used decoratively rather than technically

**What goes wrong when absent:** The claims must stand on their own stated merits, making their emptiness visible.

---

### 3. Disqualifying the Evaluative Standard

**Definition:** Pre-emptively ruling out the method by which claims would normally be assessed, insulating assertions from scrutiny.

**Examples:**
- "True understanding comes not from analysis" — eliminates analytical evaluation as legitimate
- "Rational minds can only approximate but never fully grasp" — demotes the faculty used to check claims
- "Surrendering to the flow" positioned as superior to examination

**What goes wrong when absent:** Claims remain open to the falsification or scrutiny they cannot survive.

---

### 4. Conflating Metaphor With Mechanism

**Definition:** Treating a poetic or analogical description as if it were a causal or structural explanation.

**Examples:**
- "Every atom carries the memory of creation" — memory is metaphorical, but positioned as literal atomic property
- "The dance of light and shadow reveals patterns" — aesthetic observation presented as epistemic method
- "Universal intelligence that permeates all things" — intelligence metaphor treated as ontological claim

**What goes wrong when absent:** The distinction between illustration and explanation must be respected, forcing actual mechanistic accounts.

---

### 5. Smuggling Normative Conclusions From Descriptive Premises

**Definition:** Moving from an "is" to an "ought" or from a descriptive phenomenon to a prescriptive framework without argument.

**Examples:**
- From "quantum observer effect" (descriptive physics) to "consciousness shapes reality" (sweeping ontological claim) to implied prescription: align your consciousness
- From "patterns exist" to "rational minds cannot grasp them" to implied conclusion: abandon rational analysis

**What goes wrong when absent:** The gap between what is observed and what should be done must be explicitly bridged.

---

### 6. Cascading Vagueness to Avoid Falsification

**Definition:** Using terms that shift meaning or remain undefined, so no specific claim is ever pinned down enough to be wrong.

**Examples:**
- "Fundamental frequency of existence" — frequency of what, measured how?
- "Universal intelligence" — what distinguishes its presence from its absence?
- "Infinite possibility" in every moment — what would finite possibility look like?

**What goes wrong when absent:** Claims become specific enough to be tested and potentially refuted.
