**Catalog of Reasoning Operators**

**Distinguishing separate objections**  
Isolates one argumentative thread from another to block conflation of distinct premises.  
- B explicitly flags the shift: “That’s a different objection—now you’re arguing from utility, not causation.”  
- A’s initial causal-access worry is kept apart from the later utility and preference claims.  
- Without the move, replies address the wrong premise and the exchange slides between unrelated criteria.

**Identifying hidden assumptions**  
Surfaces an unstated theoretical commitment required by the opponent’s inference.  
- B names the causal theory of knowledge as presupposed rather than defended.  
- A’s claim that inert facts are unknowable is shown to rest on a specific epistemology.  
- Absent this step, both sides argue from incompatible background theories without noticing the mismatch.

**Testing consistency across isomorphic domains**  
Applies the same logical standard to structurally parallel cases (mathematics, aesthetics) to expose selective application.  
- Math’s causal inertness is used to question whether causation is necessary for knowledge.  
- Aesthetic facts are introduced to check whether predictive utility is being required only of morality.  
- Without the move, domain-specific exceptions remain unexamined and the criterion is applied unevenly.

**Demanding explanatory coverage of phenomenology**  
Requires a position to account for the apparent features of first-person moral experience rather than dismissing them.  
- B presses that “moral progress” and the “sense that we’re discovering something” must be explained even on a deflationary view.  
- The move treats these appearances as data any adequate account must address.  
- Absent the demand, theories can declare the phenomena illusory without showing how the illusion arises or why it feels non-arbitrary.

**Checking whether an argument overgeneralizes**  
Evaluates whether a premise, if accepted, eliminates distinctions the opponent wishes to retain.  
- B notes that rejecting non-predictive facts would also eliminate aesthetic value, which A presumably accepts.  
- The move reveals that the stated criterion is stronger than intended.  
- Without it, positions are adopted that quietly undermine neighboring commitments the speaker still wants to keep.
