**Key take‑aways for applying the operators**

- *Identify hidden assumptions* ensures that every claim rests on explicitly stated premises; otherwise arguments may rest on vague or false preconceptions.

- *Reconstruct charitably* safeguards against straw‑man attacks and keeps the focus on the strongest version of the opponent’s position.

- *Distinguish descriptive vs normative* clarifies whether a claim is about facts or obligations, preventing conflation that obscures solution strategies.

- *Distinguish epistemic grounds* separates cause, utility, intuition, or other justifications, preventing over‑claiming of explanatory power.

- *Attack a specific premise* concentrates rebuttal, increasing logical force and preventing generic, diffuse criticisms.

- *Use analogous evidence* imports successful solutions from structurally similar domains, enriching the argument base.

- *Clarify phenomenology* brings the lived, experiential aspect into account, ensuring theories are not purely abstract.

- *Translate between isomorphic descriptions* identifies structural commonalities across accounts, providing unified explanatory frameworks.

- *Separate goals from optimization* decouples the purpose of the theory from the method, avoiding circularity.

- *Attack epistemic theory vs logical objection* guarantees attention to both deductive and empirical lines, preventing one‑sided defenses.
