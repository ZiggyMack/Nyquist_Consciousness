**Catalog of Reusable Reasoning Operators**

**Distinguishing metrics from one another**  
Separates distinct evaluative dimensions so that challenges or evidence relevant to one dimension are not automatically imported into another.  
Examples: Repeatedly separating BFI from MS when engaging Grant’s Syllogism; treating PS as tracking behavioral traction independently of MS justificatory success; scoring LS on internal coherence rather than demonstrated grounding.  
Absent: Scores collapse into one another; a moral or epistemic objection to one metric is allowed to nullify performance on an ontologically or practically distinct metric without argument.

**Pricing concessions incrementally**  
Adjusts an overall evaluation by quantified, localized deductions tied to specific arguments rather than global shifts.  
Examples: BFI score moves 9.2 → 8.5 → 8.4 → 8.3 with explicit deltas (−0.4 for propagation risk, −0.1 for bootstrapping); MS score moves 7.1 → 6.3 → 5.8 → 5.0 → 4.8 with listed parasitism and trajectory discounts; PS score moves 5.5 → 4.6 → 4.4 → 4.2 → 4.0 with itemized −0.3/−0.2 adjustments.  
Absent: Binary or all-or-nothing score changes that ignore partial validity of objections and produce unstable or untraceable evaluations.

**Diagnosing metric-definition disputes**  
Identifies when disagreement is about what a metric is supposed to measure rather than the underlying facts of the case.  
Examples: Explicitly naming that the BFI gap turns on whether the metric requires MS-coherence as a precondition; stating that the PS gap tracks whether the metric is grounding-dependent or tracks realized behavioral traction; flagging that the LS gap is “demonstrated grounding” versus “foundationally coherent grounding.”  
Absent: Treating definitional disagreements as ordinary factual or evidentiary disputes, leading to endless first-order argument that cannot resolve the impasse.

**Distinguishing contested from failed**  
Maintains that a claim or framework can remain philosophically live (contested among competent practitioners) without being decisively refuted (failed).  
Examples: “Tension ≠ contradiction” on the syllogism; “contested, not demonstrated” on the participation relation; “logical PoE widely considered defeated even if evidential PoE remains live.”  
Absent: Any unresolved challenge is treated as total failure, collapsing distinctions between live philosophical options and refuted positions.

**Identifying when a standard proves too much**  
Tests whether an opponent’s evaluative criterion would apply symmetrically across cases and produce implausible or uniform results.  
Examples: “Demonstration standard would score every foundational metaphysics ~3.5–4.0 on CA”; “Grok’s grounding-success demand would collapse PS for Kantianism, utilitarianism, and secular humanism alike”; “non-question-begging external resolution requirement is unfulfillable for any comprehensive framework.”  
Absent: Asymmetric or over-demanding criteria are accepted without consistency check, producing metric collapse or special pleading.
