## TIER 3: THE "NERFED EQUATIONS" ANGLE

This is the claim that Dirac's original formulations contained structure that was
simplified away when QFT replaced the Dirac sea interpretation. Three threads:

### 3A. The Dirac Sea → QFT Replacement

Dirac's negative energy sea was replaced by particle-antiparticle formalism.
Some argue this "lost" the physical picture Dirac intended.

| Source | URL | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Wikipedia: Dirac sea | https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dirac_sea | Good overview of what was replaced and why |
| Physics Forums discussion | https://www.physicsforums.com/threads/negative-energy-solutions-dirac-equation.273492/ | Active debate about what was lost |
| arxiv: Historical context of Dirac equation | https://arxiv.org/pdf/2504.17797 | Comparison with Schrödinger and Klein-Gordon |

### 3B. Hestenes' "Real Dirac Equation" — Hidden Geometric Structure

David Hestenes rewrote the Dirac equation in geometric algebra (Clifford algebra),
eliminating complex numbers entirely. He argues the imaginary unit i HIDES a
geometric meaning — it's actually the generator of rotations in the spin plane.

| Source | URL | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Hestenes: "Real Dirac Theory" (PDF) | https://davidhestenes.net/geocalc/pdf/REAL_DIRAC.pdf | The manifesto. Claims standard formulation obscures geometric meaning |
| Hestenes: "Observables, operators, and complex numbers" | https://davidhestenes.net/geocalc/pdf/Observ-opers.pdf | What's hidden by the imaginary unit |
| Hestenes: Overview of Geometric Algebra in Physics | https://davidhestenes.net/geocalc/html/Overview.html | Full framework |
| Hestenes: Clifford Algebra and Interpretation of QM | https://davidhestenes.net/geocalc/pdf/caiqm.pdf | Interpretation implications |
| "Dirac's Hidden Geometry" (Not Even Wrong blog) | https://www.math.columbia.edu/~woit/wordpress/?p=262 | Peter Woit's take |
| Quaternion formulations of Dirac equation | https://www.researchgate.net/publication/265240377 | Alternative algebraic forms |

### 3C. Dirac's Own Dissent — He Thought QED Was Wrong

Dirac never accepted renormalization. He called it "ugly" and "not mathematically
sensible." His aether paper (2D above) and his persistent pursuit of alternatives
suggest he believed the standard interpretation lost something essential.

| Source | URL | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|
| "Is There an Aether?" (see 2D above) | — | Dirac's direct challenge to the mainstream |
| arXiv: "Paul Dirac and the Pervasiveness of His Thinking" | https://arxiv.org/pdf/hep-th/0304133 | How his dissenting views influenced later physics |
| Zero Point Energy and the Dirac Equation (academia.edu) | https://www.academia.edu/93302117/Zero_Point_Energy_and_the_Dirac_Equation | Connection between Dirac sea and vacuum energy |
| "On New Physical Reality (on Psi-ether)" | https://arxiv.org/pdf/physics/0111072 | Revisiting Dirac's aether in modern context |
