## Recommended Dirac Sources (Priority Order)

### Tier 1: ESSENTIAL — Process-Revealing, Long-Form

These are the sources most likely to yield operators. Load these into NotebookLM first.

**1. "The Evolution of the Physicist's Picture of Nature" (1963)**
- Scientific American, May 1963
- Dirac's most explicit methodological statement
- Contains the famous beauty principle, "playing with equations," and his view of how physics progresses
- ~10 pages but DENSE with cognitive architecture
- Widely available, frequently reprinted

**2. "The Relation Between Mathematics and Physics" (1939)**
- James Scott Lecture, Edinburgh
- Published in Proceedings of the Royal Society of Edinburgh, 59, Part II, pp. 122-129
- His clearest statement on why mathematical beauty guides physical truth
- Predates the 1963 article — earlier, rawer version of the same ideas
- Available in various Dirac collections

**3. "Directions in Physics" (1978)**
- Lectures at University of New South Wales
- Published as a book (Wiley, 1978)
- Later career — more reflective about methodology
- Covers QED, magnetic monopoles, cosmology, and large numbers
- Good for seeing which cognitive moves persist across decades

**4. Recorded Interviews (1970s-1980s)**
- Several oral history interviews exist:
  - **Archive for History of Quantum Physics (AHQP)** interview — Bohr library
  - **Thomas Kuhn interview (1962)** — long, detailed, process-focused
  - **Filmed interview at Florida State (1980s)** — late career reflections
- These are the closest analog to the Barandes podcast — conversational, spontaneous, process-revealing
- Check: American Institute of Physics oral history collection

**5. Graham Farmelo, "The Strangest Man" (2009)**
- Biography — Faber & Faber / Basic Books
- Chapters on the Dirac equation discovery, QED, monopoles, large numbers
- Farmelo had access to Dirac's personal papers and correspondence
- Focus on chapters describing HOW Dirac worked, not just what he found
- Key chapters: ~8-12 (equation discovery), ~20+ (later methodology)

### Tier 2: HIGH VALUE — Supplementary

**6. "The Principles of Quantum Mechanics" — Preface + Chapter 1 (1930, 4th ed. 1958)**
- Dirac's own textbook
- The preface and first chapter contain methodological statements
- Shows how he ORGANIZED physics — structure reveals architecture
- Available in many libraries, frequently reprinted (Oxford University Press)

**7. Helge Kragh, "Dirac: A Scientific Biography" (1990)**
- Cambridge University Press
- More technical than Farmelo — better on the physics process
- Detailed reconstruction of how specific discoveries happened step-by-step
- Good for Q3 (Dirac equation process) and Q13 (failure modes)

**8. "Mathematical Foundations of Quantum Theory" (1978)**
- Edited by A.R. Marlow, Academic Press
- Contains a Dirac lecture on his approach
- Process-revealing in a conference setting

**9. Curt Jaimungal Interviews on Dirac**
- Check Theories of Everything / "No Time" channel
- Same interviewer as Barandes — consistent format
- May have guests discussing Dirac's methodology
- Would be the closest format match to Dig Site 002's primary source

### Tier 3: BACKGROUND — Context and Comparison

**10. Abraham Pais, "Inward Bound" (1986)**
- History of particle physics — chapters on Dirac in context
- Good for PASS 0 (historical contingency — what blocked earlier discovery?)

**11. Dirac's Nobel Lecture (1933)**
- "The Theory of Electrons and Positrons"
- Short but reveals what Dirac considered important to highlight
- Available from Nobel Prize website

**12. Freeman Dyson on Dirac**
- Several essays and lectures where Dyson describes Dirac's working style
- Dyson's "Disturbing the Universe" (1979) has relevant passages
- First-hand account of Dirac's cognitive architecture from a collaborator

**13. Peter Goddard, ed., "Paul Dirac: The Man and his Work" (1998)**
- Cambridge University Press
- Collection of essays about Dirac's methodology by physicists who knew him
- Multiple perspectives on the same cognitive architecture

## What to Look For While Sourcing

Questions to keep in mind while reading/listening — these are the highest-value extraction targets:

1. **Does Dirac ever describe "reading backward" from results?** (Tests RCI universality)
2. **What does "playing with equations" mean concretely?** (Operator extraction target)
3. **When beauty and experiment conflict, which wins?** (Selection criterion analysis)
4. **How does Dirac decide which leaps are worth taking?** (The "Illegitimate Leap" operator)
5. **What does Dirac explicitly refuse to do?** (Negative space = cognitive architecture boundary)
