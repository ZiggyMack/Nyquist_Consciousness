## Six Dead Assumptions of Quantum Physics

Each assumption has: the belief, the cognitive habit that sustained it, and the death event.

### 1. Dense Probability (The Omniscience Habit)

**Assumption:** Any reasonable probability model must supply joint probability distributions for every moment in time — "dense and comprehensive."

**Cognitive Habit:** Demanding mathematical omniscience. Created false dichotomy: Markov (all higher-order probs = lowest-order) or infinitely complex non-Markov. Nobody considered "less structure."

**Death Event:** ISP demonstrated that a model can succeed by REFUSING to specify higher-order probabilities. Sparsity is a feature, not a bug.

**Implications for other disciplines:** Any field that assumes comprehensive data collection is necessary for valid modeling may be missing simpler sparse-conditioning frameworks. CFA candidate: the assumption that every turn of deliberation must be scored.

### 2. Determinism at All Costs (Pre-Quantum Bias)

**Assumption:** Nature must be governed by exact, deterministic laws. Randomness = missing data, not fundamental reality.

**Cognitive Habit:** Treating randomness as temporary ignorance. Rooted in Curie's Principle ("a particular result requires a particular cause") and Einstein's "God does not play dice."

**Death Event:** ISP accepts indeterminism as fundamental. Demanding determinism forces "zillions of parallel universes" (Many-Worlds) — a pre-quantum assumption masquerading as post-quantum sophistication.

**Implications:** Any field clinging to deterministic models when data shows stochastic patterns may be forcing a pre-paradigm assumption onto the data.

### 3. Reification of Mathematical Tools (Map = Territory)

**Assumption:** If a piece of mathematics predicts reality perfectly, it must BE a physical object. Wave functions are "real clouds."

**Cognitive Habit:** Naive scientific realism. Confusing the representation with the thing represented. The map IS the territory.

**Death Event:** ISP strips the wave function to "appurtenance" — a mathematical artifact for epistemic data management, no more real than a Lagrangian. Graded realism replaces binary real/not-real.

**Implications:** CFA candidate: treating 1-10 scores as ontologically real cognitive states rather than representational artifacts of a measurement protocol.

### 4. Entitlement to Exact Trajectories (Epistemic Hubris)

**Assumption:** A successful physical theory must provide equations detailing the exact, continuous path between observations.

**Cognitive Habit:** Epistemic entitlement — assuming human models have the "right" to map the complete history of any system.

**Death Event:** ISP's "admission of our epistemic limitedness." Measuring a trajectory creates new division events — the act of probing destroys what you're measuring. Some things are "fundamentally empirically inaccessible."

**Implications:** Any field demanding continuous models of inherently sparse phenomena (inter-crux cognition, inter-observation behavior) may be imposing a requirement that physics itself doesn't meet.

### 5. Agnosticism as Maturity (The Dyson Trap)

**Assumption:** Confusion about foundational questions is a sign of student "immaturity." True understanding = realizing "there is nothing to be understood."

**Cognitive Habit:** "Shut up and calculate." Foundational questions are philosophical wastes of time. Cracks in the axioms are embarrassments, not research opportunities.

**Death Event:** ISP was discovered BECAUSE Barandes refused this trap. He treated the "cracks" in Dirac-von Neumann axioms as real problems, not philosophy. The discovery validates Leonard Cohen: "there's a crack in everything, that's how the light gets in."

**Implications:** "Shut up and benchmark" culture in ML/AI may similarly be hiding foundational discoveries. Fields with strong "don't question the formalism" norms are prime PASS 0 targets.

### 6. Observer as Special Category (Anthropocentric Bias)

**Assumption:** "Measurement" is a fundamentally different category of action than ordinary physics. Requires a macroscopic or conscious observer to trigger.

**Cognitive Habit:** Anthropocentric/macro-centric framing. Separates universe into "quantum things" and "measuring things" (the Category Problem).

**Death Event:** ISP eliminates the distinction entirely. Division events are triggered by ANY interaction — "could be just a particle could be just the simplest thing." No consciousness, size, or complexity requirement.

**Implications:** CFA candidate: the assumption that only "proper" adversarial deliberation produces useful scoring data. If any interaction generates conditioning times, simpler interaction formats may also be informative.
