# Remaining Unknowns: Dig Site 002 (Barandes — ISP Theory)

Questions that Dig Site 002 could NOT answer. These are assets for future work, not failures of the extraction.

---

## Physics-Internal

1. **Are ISP probabilities mathematically unique?** Could there be multiple valid sparse conditional probability structures that all reproduce quantum mechanics? ISP shows one path from vintage probabilities to Hilbert space — is it the only path?

2. **Is relational primitivism stable under larger systems?** Barandes commits to Primitivism (probabilities as governing features of reality) but admits ISP may be "an intermediate step." Does relational primitivism remain coherent when applied to systems of arbitrary complexity, or does it develop internal tensions?

3. **Does graded realism extend cleanly outside physics?** The 3-degree taxonomy (fundamental, emergent-but-real, not real) works for physical objects. Does it preserve its discriminative power when applied to consciousness, social institutions, mathematical objects, or normative facts? Or does it need domain-specific modifications?

4. **What is the exact mechanism by which teaching constraint produces discovery?** Pedagogical Forcing (OP-012) is confirmed as the catalyst for ISP, but the mechanism connecting "try to teach it simply" to "discover hidden structure" is not formalized. Under what conditions does pedagogical forcing succeed vs fail?

## Cross-Framework

5. **Does Category Theory formally reconstruct ISP?** The structural features (configurations as objects, conditional probabilities as morphisms, SQ Correspondence as functor) strongly invite categorical reformulation, but Barandes doesn't mention it. Would a categorical formulation expose structure invisible to both the probability and Hilbert space pictures?

6. **Is CFA structurally homologous or mathematically analogous? (≅ or =?)** The structural map (division events ≅ cruxes, Axiom 2 ≅ levers, etc.) is compelling, but is this genuine structural isomorphism or surface analogy? The 4 testable predictions (Q37) are necessary conditions for ISP, not sufficient. What additional evidence would move from ≅ to =?

7. **Does the ISP model predict deliberation phenomena that NO other model predicts?** The 4 Q37 predictions may follow from any sparse-conditioning architecture, not ISP specifically. What ISP-SPECIFIC prediction would distinguish "CFA is an ISP" from "CFA has ISP-like properties"?

## Methodology-Level

8. **Is the 4-step discovery sequence (Teaching → Representation → Gap → Boundary) unique to Barandes or universal?** Q33 extracted it from one thinker. Does it describe how ALL hidden-structure discoveries happen, or only Barandes-type discoveries (finding simpler things hidden by complex representations)?

9. **What is the domain of applicability of the Barandes Protocol?** The extraction methodology works well for thinkers who discover via subtraction and representation-change. Does it work for thinkers who discover via creative synthesis, empirical serendipity, or collaborative insight? Dig Site 003 (negative control) should test this.

10. **Can PASS 0 be run on itself?** If we extract the hidden assumptions of the extraction methodology, what do we find? The reflexive application may reveal biases in how we choose thinkers, design questions, or interpret answers.

## Noether Extraction (Q42-Q50)

11. **Is Reverse Constraint Inference genuinely universal or specific to subtractive discoverers?** RCI (begin with robust outputs, read backward to minimal architecture) describes Noether, Barandes, and EOS well. But does it capture Dirac's "playful deformity" (forward-generative, aesthetic, no explicit backward-reading phase)? Dirac is the strongest test case.

12. **Does Dirac's discovery architecture represent a qualitatively different type?** If so, the Noetherian Discovery Algorithm (Insight 37) is not universal — it captures one discovery TYPE (subtractive/backward-reading) but not others (generative/forward-playing). This would mean the Museum needs multiple discovery architecture templates, not one master algorithm.

13. **Are false invariants detectable BEFORE a representation shift, or only after?** Q45 shows Barandes identified false invariants by changing representation. But can the Noether Lens detect them in advance — predict which features WON'T survive before running the experiment? If yes, the Lens is predictive. If no, it's only diagnostic (still valuable, but weaker).
