# Operator 016: Anomaly Elevation

**Confidence:** RED
**First recovered:** Dig Site 003 (Dirac)
**Independent confirmations:** 1
**Extractor-independence:** Unknown
**Family:** Aesthetic Generation

---

## Definition

When a formalism produces a bizarre anomaly that violates established intuition and alarms your peers, do not patch or discard it — isolate the anomaly and elevate it to the foundational axiom of the new system.

---

## The Operation

The thinker encounters a result that looks like a fatal bug (a contradiction, an impossibility, a "flaw"). Instead of the default move (suppress it, add an epicycle, or abandon the theory), they invert the evaluation: they treat the anomaly as the most important *feature* and rebuild the theory so the anomaly is load-bearing. Input: a perceived defect. Output: a new axiom + the physics forced by it.

**Detection condition:** A community treats result X as a problem to be eliminated; the thinker treats X as the answer. Conditional — applies when a formalism yields a result that resists intuition.

**Scope:** Domain-conditional (fires at frontiers where a formalism outruns interpretation).

---

## Examples

### From Dirac (Dig Site 003)

- **Non-commutation:** Heisenberg was "scared his whole theory might collapse" when his variables failed to commute (uv ≠ vu). Dirac isolated this "flaw" and made it the master key of quantum mechanics (mapping it to the Poisson bracket).
- **Negative energy → antimatter:** rather than discard the impossible negative-energy solutions of his equation, he elevated them to reality (the "Dirac sea"), predicting the positron.

## Failure Modes

1. **Elevating noise:** the same move applied to a mere coincidence produces a false law — Dirac's Large Numbers Hypothesis elevated a numerical coincidence (varying-G). The operator does not distinguish a deep anomaly from an accident; it needs an external selection check.
2. **Premature commitment:** building a whole edifice on an anomaly that later dissolves under a better representation.
