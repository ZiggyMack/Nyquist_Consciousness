# Operator 006: Under-Determination Detection

**Confidence:** RED
**First recovered:** Dig Site 001 (Nova synthesis)
**Independent confirmations:** 1

---

## Definition

When a formalism underdetermines an outcome, any procedure that claims to determine one is importing extra structure. Identify the import.

---

## The Operation

Examine a derivation or argument. If the formal premises do not uniquely determine the conclusion — if multiple conclusions are compatible with the premises — then something beyond the premises selected the stated conclusion. That something is imported structure. Identifying it is the operation.

This is the most general operator recovered so far: OP-001 (representation ≠ ontology), OP-002 (hidden selection audit), and OP-005 (hidden structure injection) are all special cases of under-determination detection applied to specific import channels.

Input: A derivation that claims its conclusion follows from its premises.
Output: Either "premises determine conclusion — derivation is valid" or "premises underdetermine — extra structure was imported at step X."

---

## Examples

### From Nova's synthesis (Dig Site 001)

Nova identified under-determination as the common abstraction across all domains in the Adlam/Barandes transcript:
- **Quantum mechanics:** Born rule underdetermines measurement outcomes → extra structure (observer, collapse mechanism) imported
- **Personal identity:** Physics underdetermines whether teleporter preserves identity → extra structure (definition of self) imported
- **Emergence:** Microphysics underdetermines macroscopic reality → extra structure (macroscopic categories) imported
- **Credence:** Self-locating formalism underdetermines probabilities → extra structure (goals) imported
- **CFA:** Phase 1 metrics underdetermine scores (no anchors) → extra structure (auditor's definition) imported

The pattern: formalism → gap → import → conclusion that looks determined but isn't.

---

## Failure Modes

1. **Determination Illusion:** Believing a conclusion is determined when it isn't. The most widespread failure — most arguments in philosophy and science contain undeclared imports that make underdetermined conclusions look determined.
2. **Over-Determination Paranoia:** Seeing under-determination everywhere, including where conclusions genuinely follow from premises. Not every derivation is smuggling.
