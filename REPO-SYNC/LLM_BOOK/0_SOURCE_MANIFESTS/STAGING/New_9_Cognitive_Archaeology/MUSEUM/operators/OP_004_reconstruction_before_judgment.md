# Operator 004: Reconstruction Before Judgment

**Confidence:** YELLOW
**First recovered:** Dig Site 001 (Barandes + Adlam)
**Independent confirmations:** 2 (Barandes/Adlam, CFA Phase sequence)

---

## Definition

Faithfully reconstruct the object of study in its own terms before evaluating it from an external framework.

---

## The Operation

Before scoring, critiquing, comparing, or ranking something, first reconstruct it as its proponents would recognize it. Use THEIR categories, THEIR definitions, THEIR success criteria. Only after this reconstruction is complete — and verified as faithful — should external evaluation begin. Skipping reconstruction guarantees that you will evaluate a strawman version of the object, producing conclusions that are artifacts of the evaluator's framework rather than genuine assessments.

Input: An object to be evaluated (theory, framework, argument, institution, worldview).
Output: A reconstruction that the object's proponents would accept as accurate, THEN an evaluation.

---

## Examples

### From Barandes (Dig Site 001)

Barandes insists on translating quantum mechanical claims into equivalent mathematical representations before assessing them. He doesn't critique Many-Worlds from outside — he translates its claims into an isomorphic formalism and shows that the claims don't survive the translation. The critique comes FROM the reconstruction, not before it.

### From Adlam (Dig Site 001)

Adlam reconstructs the self-location puzzle in decision-theoretic terms before declaring that credences in this domain aren't beliefs. She doesn't dismiss the puzzle — she rebuilds it in precise formal language, and THEN shows that the rebuilt version dissolves.

### From CFA

The CFA Phase sequence: Phase 1a is faithful reconstruction (describe the FUT in its own terms), Phase 1b is internal coherence check (does it succeed by its own criteria?), Phase 2 is lever calibration (external evaluation). Skipping Phase 1a — jumping straight to scoring — produces the premature evaluation failure mode (FM-3).

---

## Failure Modes

1. **Premature Evaluation:** Evaluating a framework using categories it doesn't recognize. Scoring "moral substance" using a definition the framework wouldn't accept. This is the most common failure in philosophy, book reviews, and political debate.
2. **Hostile Reconstruction:** Reconstructing the object deliberately badly to make critique easier. Different from a strawman (which is unintentional) — this is strategic misrepresentation.
3. **Reconstruction Without Transition:** Doing the reconstruction well but then failing to switch to evaluation mode. Getting stuck in empathetic understanding without ever reaching a verdict.
