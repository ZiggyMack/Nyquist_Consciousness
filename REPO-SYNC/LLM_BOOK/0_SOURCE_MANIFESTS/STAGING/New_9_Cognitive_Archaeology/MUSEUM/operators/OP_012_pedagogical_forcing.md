## Definition

Use the constraint of teaching to discover structural features invisible to the expert — if you cannot reconstruct a theory from simple primitives for a novice, you probably don't understand its architecture yet.

---

## The Operation

When analyzing a complex theory or framework, attempt to teach it from first principles to someone who knows nothing about it. The constraint of translation into simple language forces you to identify: (1) what is genuinely necessary vs conventional, (2) where the standard presentation smuggles in assumptions, (3) where the "obvious" steps hide non-trivial structure.

Barandes didn't discover ISP by reading quantum information literature — he "stumbled on them" because teaching quantum axioms to students forced him to explain without hand-waving.

Nova's broader formulation: **Compression Stress Testing** — if you cannot reconstruct a theory from simple primitives, you probably don't understand its architecture yet. Teaching is one instantiation of this operator.

**Detection condition:** When expertise feels complete but cannot be articulated simply. The gap between "I understand this" and "I can explain this from scratch" is where hidden structure lives.

**Scope:** Universal — applicable to any domain where expertise exists.

---

## Examples

### From Barandes (Dig Site 002)

Teaching Harvard undergraduates forced Barandes to ask: "Why do we start with Hilbert spaces? What physical picture do they represent?" Standard pedagogy says: "These are the axioms, just learn them." The constraint of making them intuitive from scratch revealed that ordinary probabilities with sparse conditioning times could do the same work — ISP was hiding behind the conventional axiom-first presentation.

---

## Failure Modes

1. **Over-Simplification:** Simplifying until the content is wrong, not just simpler. The teaching version must reproduce the expert version, not approximate it.
2. **Expertise Blindness:** Believing your expert understanding IS simple, and concluding nothing is hidden. "It's obvious" is the most common symptom of structures you've internalized so deeply you can't see them.
