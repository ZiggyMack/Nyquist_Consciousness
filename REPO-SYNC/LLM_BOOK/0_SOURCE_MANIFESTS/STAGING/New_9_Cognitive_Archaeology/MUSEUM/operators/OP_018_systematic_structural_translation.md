# Operator 018: Systematic Structural Translation

**Confidence:** RED
**First recovered:** Dig Site 003 (Dirac)
**Independent confirmations:** 1
**Extractor-independence:** Unknown
**Family:** Translation

---

## Definition

Identify the irreducible algebraic relationships (the structural skeleton) of a successful but limited domain, discard that domain's *objects* entirely, and transplant its *relationships* intact into a new, unknown domain — forcing the new objects to obey the old rules even if their nature has changed.

---

## The Operation

Take Domain A (well-understood, elegant structure). Strip it to its relations (the algebra, the invariants). Carry those relations across a boundary into Domain B and *impose* them, letting B's objects be whatever must satisfy them. Input: a structural skeleton from A. Output: a working formalism in B. Distinct from OP-001 (which asks *which part of A is just a human picture?*); this runs *after* OP-001 and asks *how do I make the new world wear the old skeleton?*

**Detection condition:** the thinker maps a structural identity between two domains and then *systematizes* it into a translation rule (not a one-off analogy). Conditional.

**Scope:** Domain-conditional; the move itself is cross-domain by construction (candidate for wider recurrence — test in non-physics thinkers).

---

## Examples

### From Dirac (Dig Site 003)

- **Quantization via classical analogy:** recognizing that the quantum commutator (uv − vu) shares the exact structural properties of the classical **Poisson bracket**, Dirac established a direct translation rule mapping every classical Hamiltonian equation into a quantum one — "immediately pass from all the equations of classical mechanics… to new equations with noncommutative multiplication."

*Second independent example required for YELLOW.*

## Failure Modes

1. **Skeleton doesn't hold:** transplanting relations that are not actually invariant across the boundary — imposing structure B cannot bear.
2. **Object smuggling:** accidentally carrying A's *objects* along with its relations, re-importing the picture OP-001 was supposed to strip.
