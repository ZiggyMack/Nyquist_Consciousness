# Operator 002: Hidden Selection Audit

**Confidence:** RED
**First recovered:** Dig Site 001 (Barandes)
**Independent confirmations:** 1

---

## Definition

When an outcome appears determined, ask: what mechanism selected it? If no mechanism can be identified, something has been smuggled in.

---

## The Operation

Encountering any claim of the form "X naturally/obviously/inevitably happens," pause and ask: **What is doing the selecting?** If the claimant cannot identify a concrete selection mechanism — a physical process, a formal rule, a decision procedure — then the apparent determinacy is an artifact. Something (an observer, a preference, a representation choice) was imported to produce the illusion of inevitability.

Input: A claim that an outcome is natural, obvious, or determined.
Output: Either the selection mechanism (making the claim legitimate) or the absence of one (exposing the smuggle).

---

## Examples

### From Barandes (Dig Site 001)

Consciousness as "recording needle": the needle landed on one particular body — the single most important event in Barandes' existence, and one he doesn't understand. What selected this body? No known physics governs the selection. The apparent fact "I am this person" conceals an unexplained selection event.

### From Adlam (Dig Site 001)

The Born rule "selects" measurement outcomes in quantum mechanics. But what is doing the selecting? The mathematical formalism provides probabilities but no mechanism. The rule works, but the selector is invisible. Any attempt to supply the selector imports structure from outside the formalism.

---

## Failure Modes

1. **Selection Blindness:** Accepting an outcome as "natural" without noticing that a selection occurred. The most dangerous failure — invisible by construction.
2. **Mechanism Conflation:** Confusing a description of WHAT was selected with an explanation of HOW it was selected. "The wavefunction collapsed" describes the outcome; it doesn't explain the selector.
