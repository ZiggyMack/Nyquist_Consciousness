# Operator 003: Goal → Optimization Collapse

**Confidence:** RED
**First recovered:** Dig Site 001 (Adlam)
**Independent confirmations:** 1

---

## Definition

In certain domains, specifying your goal immediately and completely determines your rational strategy — no intermediate empirical or theoretical steps exist.

---

## The Operation

Identify domains where the usual chain (goal → gather evidence → build model → derive strategy) collapses. In these domains, stating the goal IS deriving the strategy. The empirical gap vanishes. This is not a shortcut — it is a structural feature of the problem space. Recognizing these domains prevents wasted effort on intermediate steps that cannot exist.

Input: A decision problem.
Output: Either "empirical gap exists — investigation needed" or "goal fixes strategy directly — no investigation possible."

---

## Examples

### From Adlam (Dig Site 001)

Pure self-locating uncertainty (cloning scenarios): "As soon as you specify your goal, the credences are already fixed." Wanting to maximize total winnings yields equal credences; wanting a specific copy to win yields probability-one. No experiment, theory, or philosophical argument can intervene. The goal IS the answer. (Q3)

### From Master→DBEP Framework

The Master/Telos layer determines the entire Description→Belief→Evaluation→Prediction stack. Once the Master is declared, the DBEP chain follows. NotebookLM independently identified this as an instance of Adlam's pattern (Q16 — reading RESEARCH_QUESTION.md and recognizing it as a 4th case).

---

## Failure Modes

1. **Empirical Mirage:** Searching for empirical evidence in a domain where goals, not evidence, determine the answer. Wasted effort at best; smuggled goals disguised as evidence at worst.
2. **Goal Smuggling:** Pretending to derive a strategy from evidence when you have actually imported a goal and let it determine the answer. The strategy appears objective but is goal-dependent.
