## Key Themes (Post-Mining)

### 1. Local-to-Global Extension Failures (Phenomenon A/B/C/D)
The core thesis as stated. Local agreement can: extend uniquely but non-trivially (A), be blocked (B), admit multiple extensions (C), or encounter self-reference (D). Sheaf theory formalizes this.

### 2. Operations Have Validity Domains (Nova's Reframe)
The deeper reading: Curt interrogates operations themselves. Reduction, aggregation, measurement, formalization, extension — each works in a domain and fails outside it. The phenomena are cases of composition operators failing.

### 3. Three Senses of Irreducibility
Compositional (no substructure), reflexive (embedded observer), conceptual (circular definition — with sub-types: chain-stops vs opaque). Hidden premises required to move between senses.

### 4. Phenomenally Thick Concepts
Understanding, character, information, measurement, agency, intelligence — concepts whose deflation simultaneously inflates the deflated. Mechanism: redefinition by equivalence ("twinly inflation").

### 5. Averted Vision / Thixotropic Consciousness
Consciousness resists formalization not because it's empty but because investigation changes its state. Structural parallel to quantum measurement (claimed as structural, not poetic).

### 6. Pre-articulate Intimation
Discovery guided by what you can't yet articulate. Cloudiness preceding the rock of formalization. Rock likely created by dredging (constructivism), not pre-existing (realism).

### 7. TOE Taxonomy (Types A-E)
Five types of "Theory of Everything" with structurally distinct ambitions, unified by mutual contempt. This IS the Reverse Elephant applied to philosophy itself.

### 8. Scale Oscillation (Nova — Observed)
Curt's methodology: constant movement between Example → Pattern → Mathematics → Example → Pattern → Philosophy. Not analogy but systematic abstraction-level cycling.
