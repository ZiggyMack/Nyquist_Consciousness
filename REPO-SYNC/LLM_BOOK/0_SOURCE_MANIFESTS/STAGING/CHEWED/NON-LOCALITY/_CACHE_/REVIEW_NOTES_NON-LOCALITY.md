## Source Summary

Comprehensive NotebookLM synthesis on quantum nonlocality: the phenomenon where entangled particles exhibit correlations that cannot be explained by local hidden variables. Traces the arc from EPR paradox (1935) through Bell's theorem (1964) to modern device-independent protocols. Key concepts: Bell inequalities, CHSH inequality (classical limit 2, quantum max 2√2 ≈ 2.828), Tsirelson's bound, device-independent quantum key distribution, and certified randomness generation.

---

## Key Insights

1. **Device-Independent Paradigm:** Security/verification from physics laws, not device trust. The violation itself IS the certificate.

2. **Tsirelson's Bound Mystery:** Quantum mechanics maxes at 2√2, not algebraic max of 4. No-signaling allows higher (PR-boxes). Suggests unknown principle constraining QM.

3. **Entanglement ≠ Nonlocality:** Necessary but not sufficient. Some entangled states have local explanations.

4. **Einstein Was Right (Sort Of):** QM IS incomplete - can't be locally realistic. But hidden variables would need to be nonlocal.
