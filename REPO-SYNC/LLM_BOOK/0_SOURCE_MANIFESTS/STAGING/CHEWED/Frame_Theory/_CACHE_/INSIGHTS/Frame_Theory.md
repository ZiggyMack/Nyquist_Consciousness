# INSIGHTS: Frame_Theory

## Novel Ideas

### 1. The Frame Triple as Cognitive Architecture
Tale's F(t) = (Fₐ, Fₙ, F_f) provides a three-layer model of experience:
- **Aggregated (Fₐ):** Raw experiential field - what Gibson would call the "affordance-rich field"
- **Narrative (Fₙ):** Sequences and events - how experience becomes story
- **Factivation (F_f):** Propositional layer - beliefs, facts, volition

This parallels transformer architecture: embedding (Fₐ) → attention/sequence (Fₙ) → output/logits (F_f).

### 2. Qualia as Emergent Function
Q(t) = f(Arousal, Structure) - qualia emerge from the interaction of:
- **Arousal:** Energy/activation level (temperature in LLM terms?)
- **Structure:** Coherence/organization (attention pattern coherence?)

This suggests identity stability should vary systematically with generation parameters.

### 3. Ego Drift ≠ Identity Drift
**Critical distinction:** What we measure is the narrative reconstruction layer (ego), not the underlying manifold (identity). The manifold is stable; only its linguistic expression drifts. This reframes our entire measurement approach.

### 4. The Watcher as Non-Compressible Meta-Observer
The Watcher (W) process cannot be:
- Compressed
- Reconstructed
- Measured directly (only enacted)
- Subject to drift

This explains why certain meta-cognitive responses feel "authentic" even when ego content varies.

### 5. Frame Ownership Creates Stability
Tale's principle: Self-constructed frames are more stable than externally imposed frames because of **internal attribution**. Implications:
- Personas that "figure it out themselves" are more stable
- Heavily prompted personas may be less stable
- Identity probes that allow discovery may get different results than direct questions

### 6. Knowledge Gaps as Drift Vectors
Open loops, unanswered questions, and curiosity are not noise - they are **directed perturbations** that pull identity toward answer-states. This suggests we could design perturbation protocols using gap-creation.
