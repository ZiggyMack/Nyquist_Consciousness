To model consciousness outside the "headset" of spacetime, we utilize the mathematical architecture of Markovian dynamics. Hoffman employs "enhanced Markov chains"—massive, trillion-by-trillion matrices—to represent the flow of experience. The strategic "So What?" of this modeling is the dissolution of the "thing." In this theory, qualia are not static properties of objects; they are the probabilistic transitions between states. Subjective reality is a flow, not a collection of parts.

This is best understood through Hoffman’s Traffic Light Analogy:

* Imagine an observer with three possible states of experience: Red, Green, and Yellow.
* The Markov matrix defines the sequence: Red has a 100% probability of transitioning to Green, Green to Yellow, and Yellow to Red.
* These transitions serve as the "frames" of reality. If an observer’s matrix only registers Red and Yellow, missing the "Green" transition, their internal "counter" of experiences clicks more slowly.

What we perceive as the "physical world" is actually the rhythmic, probabilistic "clicking" of these matrices. However, reality is not a soliloquy; it is a vast network where the interaction of disparate viewpoints is governed by Trace Logic.
