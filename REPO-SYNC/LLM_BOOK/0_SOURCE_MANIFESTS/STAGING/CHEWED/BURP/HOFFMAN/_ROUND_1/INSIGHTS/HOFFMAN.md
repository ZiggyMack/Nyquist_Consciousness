Consciousness is modeled as **Markov chains** - probabilistic transitions between experiential states:
- **States** = possible experiences (red, green, yellow for traffic light; ~10^12 for humans)
- **Transitions** = probabilities of moving from one experience to next
- **Measurable space** = $(X, \mathcal{X})$ collection of all possible experiences

**Key insight:** Physical reality emerges from the *dynamics* of experience, not vice versa.

### 2. Trace Logic: The Combination Solution (Q2-Q3)

The 40-year breakthrough: **Trace Logic** describes how observers relate:
- A "trace" is a Markov chain projected onto a subset of states
- The trace forms a **partial order** (proven by mathematician Chetan Prakash):
  - Reflexive: $P \leq P$
  - Antisymmetric: If $P \leq Q$ and $Q \leq P$, then $P \cong Q$
  - Transitive: If $P \leq Q$ and $Q \leq R$, then $P \leq R$
- The **Join** ($A \vee B$) = Least Upper Bound = minimal unified observer containing both

**This solves the combination problem** - how individual consciousnesses combine into unified experience.
