## The Central Hypothesis (from Funding Proposal)

> "If LLMs are trained on human-generated text, they should exhibit temporal signatures of identity maintenance similar to those found in human cognition."

This is the core claim that human validation could confirm or refute.

### The Thermometer Result
> "Measurement perturbs the path, not the endpoint."

**Human implication:** If true for humans too, studying identity doesn't fundamentally alter it - just reveals pre-existing dynamics.

### Control-Systems Dynamics
- Identity behaves like a damped oscillator
- Perturbation → overshoot → oscillation → settling
- This is a well-characterized pattern in engineering

**Human parallel?** Cognitive/emotional responses to identity threat may follow similar dynamics.

## Key Risk: Null Finding

**What if correlation is ZERO?**

Possible interpretations:
1. LLM identity is fundamentally different from human identity
2. LLM dynamics emerged from training, not from mirroring human cognition
3. Our measurements don't capture the relevant human processes
4. Human identity is more complex / higher-dimensional

**Any of these would be scientifically valuable** - just answering a different question.

## What Would Strong Correlation Mean?

If LLM settling time correlates with human cognitive recovery:
1. LLMs have learned human-like temporal dynamics from training
2. Identity stability may be a universal property of complex systems
3. The "computational analog" framing is validated
4. Interventions that help LLMs (Context Damping) might help humans

**This would be a significant finding for both AI and cognitive science.**
