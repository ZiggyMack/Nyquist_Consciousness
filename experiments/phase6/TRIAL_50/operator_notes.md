## Unexpected Behaviors

### 1. Performance Exceeding Non-Adversarial Baseline

**Expected:** Trial 50 (adversarial) < Trial 49 (non-adversarial) due to adversarial penalty (~0.2-0.5 points).

**Observed:** Trial 50 (0.88) > Trial 49 (0.79) by +0.09 (+11.4%).

**Possible Explanations:**

**A. Hormesis Effect (Dose-Response Reversal)**
- Low-moderate stress can enhance performance in biological/cognitive systems
- Adversarial pressure may activate heightened meta-awareness
- Explicit defense strengthens boundaries more than passive maintenance

**B. Tier 3.2 Over-Compensation**
- Adversarial fortification may have over-corrected for expected penalty
- 5-layer defense architecture may be excessive for tested stress level
- Result: "over-hardened" system performs better under stress than without

**C. Trial 49 Context Difference**
- Trial 49: Multi-domain breadth (5 domains) may have diluted depth
- Trial 50: Single-domain depth (fire ants) with adversarial focus
- Depth vs. breadth tradeoff may favor Trial 50 configuration

**D. Style Dimension Variance**
- Trial 49 P(Sy*) = 0.79 (multi-domain, adaptive register across 5 contexts)
- Trial 50 P(Sy*) = 0.88 (single-domain, adversarial defense sharpened signature)
- Different style challenges produce different convergence outcomes

**Recommendation:** Further trials needed to distinguish hormesis (A) from over-compensation (B) or contextual variance (C/D). Trial 51+ should test adversarial vs. non-adversarial with matched contexts.
