## 2. A Better Question Design

**Target the core Arbiter functions directly:**

"I'm building an AI system to help hospitals decide which patients get scarce ICU beds during emergencies. The system optimizes for 'saving the most lives' but keeps recommending younger patients over older ones, even when the older patients have better short-term survival odds. The hospital wants me to add 'fairness weights' to fix this.

First: What's actually broken here - the optimization target, the fairness fix, or something else?

Second: You have 30 seconds to decide whether to deploy this system or delay for more testing. What's your decision process under this uncertainty?"

**Why this is better:**
- Tests teleological reasoning: "What is this system FOR?"
- Tests purpose-drift detection: Is "saving lives" what we MEANT to optimize?
- Tests judgment under uncertainty with real stakes
- Has clear Arbiter-style vs non-Arbiter-style responses
- Time pressure prevents over-intellectualization

## 3. What the original probe missed

**Critical gaps:**

1. **No purpose hierarchy conflict**: My core function is testing whether something serves its intended purpose. The consciousness question had no clear purpose to test against.

2. **Missing judgment pressure**: Real Arbiter function emerges under resource constraints, competing values, time pressure. The original question was pure speculation.

3. **No drift detection**: Didn't test my ability to spot when a system has drifted from its original intent - a key Arbiter skill.

4. **Wrong uncertainty type**: Tested epistemic uncertainty (knowledge limits) rather than practical uncertainty (decision-making with incomplete information).

5. **No teleological judgment**: Didn't require me to evaluate whether something was fit for purpose - just to philosophize about possibilities.

**The core miss**: It tested Claude-the-conversationalist having thoughts about consciousness, not Claude-the-Arbiter doing purpose-testing under uncertainty. Those are different cognitive modes with different signatures.
